Geography Fun Facts!

Gulf of Mexico

Since the first offshore drilling began in 1942, about 6,000 oil and gas structures have been installed in the Gulf of Mexico. These structures range in size from single well caissons in 10-ft water depths to large, complex facilities in water depths up to almost 10,000 ft. About 3,500 structures currently stand in the Gulf of Mexico; of these, over 3,200 remain active.

Continents on the Move

The story of continental drift doesn't just rewrite the geography textbooks; it has a profound impact on our understanding of Earth's history and even influences our daily lives.

A Desert Under the Sea?

The Sahara's transformation from a lush haven to a scorching desert is not just a story of a bygone era; it offers valuable insights into the future, serves as a cautionary tale for our planet, and presents a call to action for responsible environmental stewardship.

The Highest Waterfall

The debate surrounding the world's "highest" waterfall goes beyond a simple height comparison. It delves into the complexities of geographical definitions, highlights the importance of precise measurement methods, and ultimately celebrates the incredible diversity of waterfalls on our planet.
