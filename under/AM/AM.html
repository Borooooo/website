"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Armenia

Lake Sevan

Known as the "Jewel of Armenia," Lake Sevan is one of the largest freshwater high-altitude lakes in the world. Surrounded by stunning mountain scenery, the lake offers breathtaking views and a serene atmosphere. Visitors can explore its shores, indulge in water activities like swimming and boating, or simply relax and enjoy the tranquil surroundings.

Garni Gorge and the Symphony of Stones

Located near the village of Garni, this natural wonder features unique basalt columns resembling a symphony of organ pipes. Carved by the elements over thousands of years, these hexagonal columns create an awe-inspiring sight against the backdrop of the gorge. Visitors can hike through the gorge, marvel at the geological formations, and learn about the area's rich history and mythology.

Dilijan National Park

Often referred to as Armenia's "Little Switzerland," Dilijan National Park is a lush forested area known for its pristine nature and diverse flora and fauna. The park offers countless hiking trails, picturesque landscapes, and crystal-clear streams and rivers. Visitors can explore traditional villages, visit historic monasteries, and rejuvenate in the fresh mountain air.

Jermuk Waterfall

Cascading gracefully from a height of 70 meters, Jermuk Waterfall is a natural wonder located near the spa town of Jermuk. Surrounded by lush greenery and rugged cliffs, the waterfall provides a refreshing retreat for nature lovers and photographers alike. Visitors can hike to the base of the waterfall, picnic in the surrounding area, and enjoy the soothing sound of cascading water.

Noravank Canyon

Nestled within the stunning red rocks of Armenia's Vayots Dzor Province, Noravank Canyon is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The canyon is home to the medieval Noravank Monastery, perched dramatically on a cliffside overlooking the rugged landscape. Visitors can admire the monastery's intricate architecture, explore nearby caves and rock form ations, and soak in the breathtaking views of the canyon below.

Tatev Monastery and the Wings of Tatev

Situated on a rocky plateau overlooking the Vorotan River Gorge, Tatev Monastery is one of Armenia's most iconic landmarks. Dating back to the 9th century, the monastery is renowned for its architectural beauty and spiritual significance. Visitors can ride the "Wings of Tatev," the world's longest reversible aerial tramway, to reach the monastery and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Mount Aragats

As Armenia's highest peak, Mount Aragats offers spectacular views and diverse landscapes for outdoor enthusiasts. The mountain is a popular destination for hiking, mountaineering, and camping, with trails leading to its four distinct peaks. Visitors can explore alpine meadows, volcanic craters, and ancient petroglyphs while taking in the majestic beauty of the Armenian highlands.

Khosrov Forest State Reserve

Spanning over 29,000 hectares, Khosrov Forest State Reserve is a protected area rich in biodiversity and natural beauty. Home to diverse ecosystems, including forests, grasslands, and rocky slopes, the reserve provides habitat for numerous rare and endangered species. Visitors can explore its network of hiking trails, spot wildlife such as Caucasian leopards and Bezoar goats, and immerse themselves in the tranquility of Armenia's wilderness.
