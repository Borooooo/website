GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Argentina

Iguazú Falls

Nestled within the lush rainforests of the Argentina-Brazil border, Iguazú Falls is a breathtaking natural wonder. The falls consist of 275 individual cascades, creating an awe-inspiring spectacle of water plunging into the depths below. Visitors can explore the surrounding national park, home to diverse flora and fauna, and witness the powerful force of nature from various viewpoints.

Perito Moreno Glacier

Located in the Los Glaciares National Park in Argentine Patagonia, Perito Moreno Glacier is a majestic expanse of ice surrounded by towering peaks. This advancing glacier is one of the few in the world that continues to grow, creating a dynamic landscape of ice formations and turquoise-blue lakes. Visitors can take boat tours or trek along designated trails to marvel at the glacier's immense beauty up close.

Bariloche

Situated in the picturesque Andes Mountains of northern Patagonia, Bariloche is renowned for its stunning alpine scenery and pristine lakes. Visitors can explore the Nahuel Huapi National Park, where rugged mountains give way to dense forests and crystal-clear waters. Outdoor enthusiasts can partake in activities such as hiking, skiing, or simply soaking in the serene natural surroundings.

Tierra del Fuego National Park

Located at the southernmost tip of South America, Tierra del Fuego National Park is a wild and rugged wilderness that encompasses a diverse range of landscapes, from snow-capped peaks to windswept coastlines. Visitors can embark on scenic hikes through ancient forests, paddle along serene rivers, or cruise through the Beagle Channel to witness colonies of sea lions and penguins.

Salinas Grandes

Spanning the provinces of Salta and Jujuy in northwestern Argentina, Salinas Grandes is a vast salt flat that stretches as far as the eye can see. This otherworldly landscape, reminiscent of the famous Salar de Uyuni in Bolivia, shimmers under the sun, creating a surreal and mesmerizing vista. Visitors can drive or hike across the salt flats, marveling at the stark beauty of this natural phenomenon.

Quebrada de Humahuaca

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Quebrada de Humahuaca is a stunning gorge that winds its way through the Andes Mountains of northern Argentina. This ancient geological formation boasts towering cliffs, colorful rock formations, and traditional indigenous villages, offering visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the region. Travelers can explore the area on foot or by car, stopping to admire the breathtaking vistas and historic landmarks along the way.

Patagonia

Spanning the southern regions of Argentina and Chile, Patagonia is a vast and untamed wilderness renowned for its rugged beauty and pristine landscapes. From towering granite peaks to expansive glaciers and turquoise lakes, this remote region offers endless opportunities for adventure and exploration. Visitors can trek through the iconic Torres del Paine National Park, cruise past towering icebergs in the southern fjords, or simply marvel at the awe-inspiring scenery from the comfort of a cozy lodge.

Talampaya National Park

Located in the arid region of La Rioja Province, Talampaya National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famed for its stunning red rock formations and ancient petroglyphs. The park's rugged canyons and towering cliffs provide a glimpse into the geological history of the area, while its unique flora and fauna thrive in the harsh desert environment. Visitors can take guided tours to explore the park's scenic trails and learn about its cultural significance.
