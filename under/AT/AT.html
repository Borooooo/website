GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Austria

Hallstatt

: Nestled amidst the breathtaking Salzkammergut region, Hallstatt is often referred to as one of the most picturesque villages in Austria. Surrounded by towering mountains and overlooking the tranquil Hallstätter See, this charming lakeside town captivates visitors with its quaint streets, historic architecture, and idyllic setting. Hallstatt is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning alpine landscapes.

Grossglockner High Alpine Road

Winding its way through the heart of the Austrian Alps, the Grossglockner High Alpine Road is a scenic marvel that offers unparalleled views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers. This iconic mountain pass traverses rugged terrain, passing through picturesque valleys, alpine meadows, and cascading waterfalls along the way. Visitors can drive or cycle the route, stopping at panoramic viewpoints to admire the awe-inspiring beauty of the Hohe Tauern National Park.

Lake Wolfgangsee

Tucked away in the Salzkammergut region, Lake Wolfgangsee is a sparkling gem surrounded by verdant forests and majestic mountains. With its crystal-clear waters and charming lakeside villages, this picturesque lake is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. Visitors can take leisurely boat cruises, go swimming or hiking along the scenic shoreline, or simply relax and soak in the serene atmosphere of this enchanting destination.

Zell am See

Situated on the shores of Lake Zell and framed by the towering peaks of the Austrian Alps, Zell am See is a postcard-perfect destination that epitomizes the beauty of the Salzburg region. This charming lakeside town offers a wealth of outdoor activities year-round, from skiing and snowboarding in the winter to hiking, cycling, and water sports in the summer. Visitors can also explore the town's historic center, dotted with quaint shops, cafes, and medieval buildings.

Gruner See (Green Lake)

Located in the Hochschwab Mountains of Styria, Gruner See is a natural wonder that transforms from a tranquil lake into a submerged paradise during the spring thaw. As the snow melts, the surrounding mountains feed the lake, causing its waters to rise and submerge the surrounding parkland. This unique phenomenon creates a stunning underwater landscape, with submerged trees, benches, and footpaths visible beneath the crystal-clear waters, making it a diver's paradise and a photographer's dream.

Krimml Waterfalls

Tumbling from a height of 380 meters, the Krimml Waterfalls are the highest waterfall in Austria and one of the most impressive in Europe. Located in the Hohe Tauern National Park, these cascading waters create a breathtaking spectacle amidst the rugged alpine landscape. Visitors can hike along well-maintained trails that offer stunning viewpoints of the falls, feeling the cool mist on their faces as they marvel at the sheer power and beauty of nature.

Dachstein Ice Caves

Hidden beneath the majestic Dachstein Massif in Upper Austria, the Dachstein Ice Caves are a mesmerizing underground wonderland of icy formations and glistening chambers. Exploring these natural ice sculptures is like stepping into another world, as visitors traverse narrow passages and expansive caverns adorned with intricate ice formations. Guided tours lead visitors through the labyrinthine caves, revealing the beauty and wonder of this frozen subterranean landscape.

Hintersee Lake

Tucked away in the Salzkammergut region, Hintersee Lake is a serene oasis of natural beauty surrounded by dense forests and rugged mountains. This tranquil alpine lake is renowned for its emerald-green waters, which reflect the surrounding peaks and forests like a mirror. Visitors can hike along scenic trails that wind through the picturesque countryside, pausing to admire the stunning vistas and abundant wildlife that call this idyllic destination home.
