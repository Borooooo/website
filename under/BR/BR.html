GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Brazil

Iguazu Falls

Iguazu Falls, located on the border between Brazil and Argentina, is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in the world. The thundering cascade of water plunges over towering cliffs, creating a breathtaking spectacle surrounded by lush rainforest.

Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon Rainforest, often referred to as the "lungs of the planet," is the largest tropical rainforest in the world and home to unparalleled biodiversity. This vast wilderness harbors countless species of plants and animals, including jaguars, monkeys, and vibrant birds.

Fernando de Noronha

Fernando de Noronha, an archipelago off the northeastern coast of Brazil, is a paradise of pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life. This protected marine park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a haven for snorkelers and divers, offering the chance to swim with sea turtles, dolphins, and colorful fish in the warm waters of the Atlantic Ocean

Lençóis Maranhenses National Park

Lençóis Maranhenses National Park, located in northeastern Brazil, is a surreal landscape of rolling sand dunes and freshwater lagoons that appear like mirages in the desert. During the rainy season, the depressions between the dunes fill with rainwater, creating a series of ephemeral lagoons that reflect the sky and surrounding scenery

Chapada dos Veadeiros

Chapada dos Veadeiros National Park, situated in the Brazilian Cerrado biome, is a haven for nature lovers and adventurers alike. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is known for its dramatic canyons, towering waterfalls, and ancient rock formations.

Pantanal Wetlands

The Pantanal, the world's largest tropical wetland, sprawls across the Brazilian states of Mato Grosso and Mato Grosso do Sul, offering unparalleled opportunities for wildlife viewing. This vast floodplain is home to an astonishing array of wildlife, including jaguars, giant otters, and caimans, as well as over 650 species of birds.

Chapada Diamanti

Chapada Diamantina National Park, located in the heart of Bahia state, is a paradise of rugged mountains, deep canyons, and cascading waterfalls. This protected area is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering opportunities for hiking, rock climbing, and caving amidst stunning scenery

Foz do Iguaçu National Park

Foz do Iguaçu National Park, located on the Brazilian side of Iguazu Falls, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a marvel of natural beauty. In addition to the iconic waterfalls, the park is home to lush subtropical forests teeming with wildlife, including toucans, butterflies, and coatis. Visitors can take scenic walks along well-maintained trails, enjoy panoramic views of the falls
