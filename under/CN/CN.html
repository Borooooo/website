GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of China

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, located in Hunan Province, is famous for its towering sandstone pillars, lush forests, and mist-shrouded mountains. The park's stunning landscape served as inspiration for the floating mountains in the movie "Avatar

Yellow Mountain (Huangshan)

Yellow Mountain, located in Anhui Province, is renowned for its dramatic granite peaks, twisted pine trees, and sea of clouds. The mountain's ethereal beauty has inspired countless poets and artists throughout Chinese history. Visitors can hike along scenic

Jiuzhaigou

Jiuzhaigou Valley, located in Sichuan Province, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its crystal-clear lakes, multi-tiered waterfalls, and colorful autumn foliage. The valley's pristine natural beauty and vibrant ecosystems attract visitors from around the world.

Li River and Karst Mountains

The Li River, winding through Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, is renowned for its picturesque scenery of karst limestone mountains, rice paddies, and bamboo groves. The river is dotted with iconic landmarks such as Elephant Trunk Hill and Xingping Fishing Village

Mount Everest (Qomolangma)

Mount Everest, the world's highest peak, straddles the border between China and Nepal in the Himalayas. The mountain's majestic snow-capped summit has captivated mountaineers and adventurers for centuries. Visitors can admire the awe-inspiring views of Everest from Everest Base Camp in Tibet,

Yangtze River Three Gorges

The Yangtze River Three Gorges, located in Hubei Province, is a breathtaking river canyon renowned for its towering cliffs, emerald waters, and scenic landscapes. The gorges, named Qutang, Wu, and Xiling, are best explored by cruise ship

Yunnan Stone Forest (Shilin)

Yunnan Stone Forest, located in Yunnan Province, is a surreal landscape of limestone formations resembling a forest of stone pillars. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a geological wonder, with towering karst formations, caves, and underground rivers

Sanya and the Tropical Paradise of Hainan Island

Sanya, located on Hainan Island in southern China, is known for its pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and lush tropical landscapes. This coastal paradise offers a range of outdoor activities, including swimming, snorkeling, and water sports. Visitors can relax on sandy beaches
