GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Columbia

Tayrona National Natural Park

Tayrona National Natural Park, located along Colombia's Caribbean coast, is known for its stunning beaches, lush rainforests, and diverse ecosystems. The park's pristine coastline features turquoise waters, golden sands

Cocora Valley

Cocora Valley, situated in the Andean region near the town of Salento, is famed for its towering wax palm trees, the tallest palm species in the world. The valley is surrounded by verdant hills, cloud forests, and misty landscapes, creating a surreal and picturesque setting.

Caño Cristales (River of Five Colors)

Caño Cristales, often referred to as the "River of Five Colors" or the "Liquid Rainbow," is located in the Serranía de la Macarena National Park. During the wet season (usually from June to November), the river's unique aquatic plants create a mesmerizing display of vibrant colors, including red, green, blue, and yellow.

Amazon Rainforest

Colombia is home to a portion of the Amazon Rainforest, one of the most biodiverse regions on Earth. The Colombian Amazon is characterized by dense jungles, winding rivers, and an incredible array of plant and animal species.

Los Nevados National/p>

Los Nevados National Natural Park, located in the Andean region, is characterized by snow-capped peaks, alpine lakes, and rugged terrain. The park is home to Colombia's highest mountain range, including the towering Nevado del Ruiz volcano.

San Andres and Providencia Islands

San Andres and Providencia Islands, located in the Caribbean Sea off the coast of Nicaragua, are Colombian territories known for their pristine beaches, coral reefs, and turquoise waters. The islands offer excellent opportunities for snorkeling, diving, and water sports, with vibrant coral gardens teeming with marine life.

Ciudad Perdida

\Ciudad Perdida, or the Lost City, is an ancient archaeological site hidden deep in the jungles of the Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta mountain range. Built by the indigenous Tayrona civilization around 800 AD, the site predates Machu Picchu and is considered one of Colombia's most important archaeological discoveries

Chicamocha Canyon

Chicamocha Canyon, also known as Colombia's Grand Canyon, is a spectacular natural wonder located in the Andean region near the city of Bucaramanga. The canyon is carved by the Chicamocha River, with dramatic cliffs plunging over 2,000 meters (6,600 feet) into the abyss below.
