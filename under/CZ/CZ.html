A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Czechia

Bohemian Switzerland National Park

Bohemian Switzerland National Park, located in the northwestern part of Czechia near the German border, is known for its striking sandstone formations, deep gorges, and lush forests. The park's iconic Pravčická brána (Pravčice Gate) is the largest natural sandstone arch in Europe

Šumava National Park

Šumava National Park, situated in the southwestern part of Czechia near the border with Germany and Austria, is a vast wilderness of dense forests, serene lakes, and rolling hills. The park is home to diverse wildlife, including lynx, wolves, and golden eagles.

Moravian Karst

Moravian Karst, located in the eastern part of Czechia near the city of Brno, is a captivating karst landscape characterized by limestone caves, underground rivers, and rocky gorges. The area is home to over 1,100 caves, including the famous Punkva Caves and the Macocha Abyss

Adršpach-Teplice Rocks

Adršpach-Teplice Rocks, located in the northeastern part of Czechia near the town of Adršpach, are a stunning labyrinth of sandstone formations, towering pillars, and hidden caves.

Czech Switzerland

Czech Switzerland, located in the northwestern part of Czechia along the border with Germany, is a picturesque region of sandstone cliffs, deep forests, and meandering rivers. The region is home to the romantic Pravčická brána (Pravčice Gate), the largest natural sandstone arch in Europe

Krkonoše National Park

Krkonoše National Park, situated in the northeastern part of Czechia along the border with Poland, is home to the highest mountain range in the country. The park's rugged peaks, alpine meadows, and glacial lakes create a stunning backdrop for outdoor activities such as hiking

Podyjí National Park

Podyjí National Park, located in the southeastern part of Czechia along the border with Austria, is a tranquil oasis of forests, meadows, and river valleys. The park is known for its rich biodiversity, including rare plant species and protected habitats for wildlife.

Bohemian Paradise

Bohemian Paradise, located in the northern part of Czechia, is a captivating landscape of sandstone rock formations, medieval castles, and dense forests. The region is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering opportunities for hiking
