A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Estonia

Lahemaa National Park

Lahemaa National Park, located in northern Estonia along the Gulf of Finland, is the country's largest national park and a haven for nature lovers. The park boasts diverse landscapes, including coastal cliffs, sandy beaches, and dense forests, as well as historic manor houses and traditional fishing villages

Soomaa National Park

Soomaa National Park, situated in southwestern Estonia, is known as the "Land of Bogs" and is characterized by its unique wetland ecosystems and pristine wilderness areas. The park is home to numerous rivers, lakes, and vast expanses of peat bogs, providing habitat for diverse plant and animal species

Tallinn Old Town

Tallinn Old Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a charming medieval city located on the northern coast of Estonia. The Old Town's cobblestone streets, historic buildings, and picturesque squares make it a popular destination for visitors seeking culture and history.

Panga Cliff

Panga Cliff, situated on the island of Saaremaa in western Estonia, is the highest natural cliff in the country, offering stunning views of the Baltic Sea and surrounding coastline. The cliff is known for its rugged beauty, with sheer limestone walls rising up to 21 meters above sea level.

Lahemaa Peninsula

Lahemaa Peninsula, located within Lahemaa National Park, is a scenic coastal region known for its sandy beaches, rocky shores, and ancient forests. The peninsula is home to historic lighthouses, traditional fishing villages, and picturesque manor houses surrounded by landscaped gardens.

Tuhala Witch's Well:

Tuhala Witch's Well, located in central Estonia near the village of Tuhala, is a unique natural phenomenon where water bubbles up from an underground river, creating a mysterious and enchanting spectacle

Kuressaare Castle

Kuressaare Castle, located on the island of Saaremaa, is one of the best-preserved medieval castles in the Baltic region and a symbol of Estonia's rich history and heritage. The castle dates back to the 14th century and is surrounded by a moat, fortified walls, and picturesque gardens.

Põhja-Kõrvemaa Nature Reserve

Põhja-Kõrvemaa Nature Reserve, located in northern Estonia, is a vast wilderness area known for its pristine forests, pristine lakes, and diverse wildlife. The reserve offers opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife photography
