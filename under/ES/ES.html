GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Spain

The Canary Islands

The Canary Islands, located off the northwest coast of Africa, boast diverse landscapes ranging from volcanic peaks to sandy beaches. Each island offers its own unique charm, with highlights including Tenerife's Mount Teide, the highest peak in Spain, and Gran Canaria's stunning sand dunes of Maspalomas.

The Pyrenees Mountains

The Pyrenees, stretching along the border between Spain and France, are a majestic mountain range characterized by rugged peaks, lush valleys, and picturesque villages. Visitors can hike through stunning landscapes, ski in world-class resorts such as Baqueira-Beret and La Molina, and explore cultural landmark

Sierra Nevada National Park

Sierra Nevada National Park, located in the southern region of Andalusia, is home to mainland Spain's highest peak, Mulhacén, and a diverse range of ecosystems, including alpine meadows, pine forests, and glacial lakes.

Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park

Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park, situated on the southeastern coast of Spain, is a rugged and pristine coastal area known for its dramatic cliffs, hidden coves, and crystal-clear waters. Visitors can explore secluded beaches, snorkel among vibrant coral reefs, and hike along scenic coastal trails offering panoramic views of the Mediterranean Sea

Picos de Europa National Park

Picos de Europa National Park, located in the northern region of Asturias, Cantabria, and Castile and León, is a rugged and mountainous wilderness known for its towering peaks, deep gorges, and lush forests.

Montserrat Mountain

Montserrat, located near Barcelona in Catalonia, is a unique mountain range renowned for its distinctive rock formations and Benedictine monastery, Santa Maria de Montserrat. Visitors can take a cable car or hike to the monastery

Cazorla, Segura y Las Villas Natural Park

Cazorla, Segura y Las Villas Natural Park, located in the eastern region of Andalusia, is the largest protected area in Spain and a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. The park is known for its rugged limestone mountains, deep river valleys, and dense forests teeming with wildlife.
