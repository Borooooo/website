A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Ethopia

Simien Mountains National Park

Simien Mountains National Park, located in the northern part of Ethiopia, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its dramatic landscapes, rugged peaks, and deep valleys. The park is home to diverse wildlife, including the endemic Walia ibex and Ethiopian wolf.

Danakil Depression

The Danakil Depression, located in northeastern Ethiopia, is one of the hottest and lowest places on Earth, with surreal landscapes of salt flats, volcanic craters, and colorful geothermal pools. The depression is home to the otherworldly Erta Ale volcano, which hosts a continuously active lava lake.

Lake Tana

Lake Tana, located in the northern highlands of Ethiopia, is the largest lake in the country and the source of the Blue Nile River. The lake is surrounded by lush vegetation, papyrus swamps, and historic monasteries dating back to the 14th century

Bale Mountains National Park

Bale Mountains National Park, located in southeastern Ethiopia, is a diverse and pristine wilderness area known for its highland plateaus, deep valleys, and Afro-alpine moorlands. The park is home to endemic wildlife species such as the Ethiopian wolf, mountain nyala, and Bale monkey

The Blue Nile Falls (Tis Issat)

The Blue Nile Falls, also known as Tis Issat or "Smoking Water," is a majestic waterfall located on the Blue Nile River in northern Ethiopia. The falls plunge over a series of basalt cliffs, creating a spectacular display of mist and rainbows.

The Rift Valley Lake

The Rift Valley Lakes, located in the Great Rift Valley of Ethiopia, are a series of stunning freshwater lakes known for their scenic beauty and rich biodiversity. Lakes such as Lake Ziway, Lake Langan

The Semien Shewa Zone

The Semien Shewa Zone, located in the central highlands of Ethiopia, is characterized by rolling hills, fertile valleys, and traditional agricultural landscapes. The region is home to historic towns such as Lalibela, known for its rock-hewn churches dating back to the 12th century.

Awash National Park

Awash National Park, located in the Great Rift Valley of northeastern Ethiopia, is a diverse wilderness area known for its wildlife, including elephants, lions, and over 450 bird species. The park is dominated by the Awash River, which forms waterfalls and deep gorges as it flows through the landscape
