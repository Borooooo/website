GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Finland

Lapland

Lapland, located in the northernmost region of Finland, is renowned for its stunning wilderness, including vast forests, pristine lakes, and snow-covered landscapes. Visitors can experience the magic of the Northern Lights dancing across the night sky, embark on husky safaris or reindeer sleigh ride

Nuuksio National Park

Nuuksio National Park, situated near Helsinki in southern Finland, is a tranquil oasis of forests, lakes, and rocky cliffs. The park's hiking trails lead visitors through ancient woodlands, past crystal-clear streams, and to scenic viewpoints overlooking the surrounding landscapes.

The Archipelago Sea

The Archipelago Sea, located off the southwest coast of Finland, is a breathtaking mosaic of thousands of islands and islets scattered across the Baltic Sea. Visitors can explore the archipelago by boat, kayak, or ferry, hopping from one picturesque island to another.

Lemmenjoki National Park

Lemmenjoki National Park, located in Finnish Lapland, is the largest national park in Finland and a pristine wilderness area rich in Sami culture and tradition. The park is home to the Lemmenjoki River, which winds its way through deep valleys and ancient forests, providing habitat for reindeer, brown bears, and rare bird species

Koli National Park

Koli National Park, situated in eastern Finland's North Karelia region, is famous for its stunning landscapes of forested hills, rugged cliffs, and panoramic views over Lake Pielinen. Visitors can hike to the summit of Ukko-Koli, the park's highest peak, for breathtaking vistas of the surrounding countryside

Pyhä-Luosto National Park

Pyhä-Luosto National Park, located in Finnish Lapland, is characterized by its ancient forests, rugged fells, and dramatic landscapes shaped by glaciers and erosion. The park is home to the Pyhä-Luosto fell chain

Oulanka National Park

Oulanka National Park, situated in northeastern Finland near the Russian border, is known for its breathtaking river valleys, rapids, and pristine wilderness. The park's iconic suspension bridge, hanging over the Oulanka River, offers stunning views of the canyon below.

Repovesi National Park

Repovesi National Park, located in southeastern Finland, is a picturesque landscape of crystal-clear lakes, rugged cliffs, and dense forests. Visitors can explore the park's network of hiking trails, which lead to scenic viewpoints, suspension bridges, and historic logging sites
