GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Gabon

Loango National Park

Loango National Park, located along the Atlantic coast in western Gabon, is a pristine wilderness area known for its diverse ecosystems, including mangrove forests, savannas, and pristine beaches.

Ivindo National Park

Ivindo National Park, located in northeastern Gabon, is a remote and untouched wilderness area renowned for its stunning waterfalls, dense rainforests, and pristine rivers. The park is home to the iconic Kongou Falls, one of the tallest waterfalls in Africa, as well as diverse wildlife, including chimpanzees, forest elephants

Pongara National Park

Pongara National Park, situated on the coast near Libreville, the capital city of Gabon, is a coastal reserve known for its sandy beaches, mangrove forests, and rich biodiversity. The park is home to diverse bird species, including pelicans, flamingos

Lopé National Park

Lopé National Park, located in central Gabon, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the country's largest protected areas, known for its diverse landscapes, including dense rainforests, savannas, and rivers.

Crystal Mountains

The Crystal Mountains, located in northeastern Gabon near the border with Cameroon, are a stunning geological formation characterized by towering granite peaks, lush forests, and crystal-clear streams.

Mayumba National

Mayumba National Park, located on the southern coast of Gabon, is a coastal reserve known for its pristine beaches, mangrove forests, and diverse marine life. The park is home to nesting sea turtles, including leatherback and olive ridley turtles, as well as rare bird species such as the African penguin and African grey parrot

Igue Misséré

Igue Misséré Forest Reserve, located in southeastern Gabon near the border with Congo, is a protected area known for its lush rainforests, pristine rivers, and diverse wildlife. The reserve is home to rare and endangered species

Akanda National Park

Akanda National Park, located near Libreville on the coast of Gabon, is a coastal reserve known for its mangrove forests, tidal estuaries, and rich birdlife. The park is home to diverse bird species, including kingfishers
