GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Greenland

Ilulissat Icefjord

lulissat Icefjord, located on the west coast of Greenland, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its stunning icebergs and towering glaciers. Visitors can take boat tours to navigate among the colossal ice formations, marvel at the dramatic calving events, and witness the sheer beauty and power of nature in action.

Disko Island

Disko Island, situated off the west coast of Greenland, is known for its rugged coastlines, towering mountains, and vibrant Inuit culture. Visitors can explore the island's scenic hiking trails, discover ancient Inuit settlements, and witness the majestic beauty of the Northern Lights dancing across the night sky.

Greenland Ice Sheet

The Greenland Ice Sheet, covering approximately 80% of the island's landmass, is the second-largest ice sheet in the world after Antarctica. Visitors can embark on guided excursions to explore the ice sheet's vast expanse

Nuuk Fjord

Nuuk Fjord, located near the capital city of Nuuk on the southwest coast of Greenland, is a scenic fjord surrounded by towering mountains, glaciers, and pristine wilderness. Visitors can take boat tours to explore the fjord's crystal-clear waters

Qeqertarsuaq (Godhavn)

Qeqertarsuaq, formerly known as Godhavn, is the largest town on Disko Island and a gateway to Greenland's Arctic wonders. Visitors can hike to nearby hot springs, explore colorful Inuit settlements, and marvel at the island's rugged coastline and towering basalt cliffs.

Kangerlussuaq

Kangerlussuaq, located in western Greenland, is a remote outpost surrounded by vast tundra, mountains, and glaciers. Visitors can explore the nearby Russell Glacier, go hiking in the pristine wilderness, and enjoy birdwatching and wildlife spotting in the Arctic landscape.

Aasiaat Archipelago

The Aasiaat Archipelago, situated off the west coast of Greenland, is a stunning network of islands, fjords, and icebergs. Visitors can explore the archipelago on boat tours, kayak expeditions, or hiking adventures, discovering hidden coves, towering cliffs, and pristine beaches along the way.

Eqi Glacier

Eqi Glacier, located near Ilulissat on the west coast of Greenland, is a breathtaking natural wonder known for its towering ice cliffs and spectacular calving events. Visitors can take boat tours to approach the glacier's edge, witness massive icebergs breaking off into the sea, and listen to the thunderous roar of the ice
