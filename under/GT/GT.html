GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Guatemala

Tikal National Park

Tikal National Park, located in the northern region of Guatemala, is home to the ancient Mayan ruins of Tikal, nestled within a lush tropical rainforest. Visitors can explore the towering pyramids, temples, and palaces of this UNESCO World Heritage Site

Lake Atitlán

Lake Atitlán, situated in the Guatemalan Highlands, is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, surrounded by towering volcanoes, lush forests, and indigenous Mayan villages. Visitors can take boat tours across the lake, hike along scenic trails

Semuc Champey

Semuc Champey, located in the Alta Verapaz region of Guatemala, is a natural limestone bridge spanning the Cahabón River, creating a series of turquoise pools cascading down terraced tiers. Visitors can swim in the crystal-clear waters, hike through lush forests

Pacaya Volcano

Pacaya Volcano, one of Guatemala's most active volcanoes, offers breathtaking views of smoking craters, lava fields, and panoramic vistas of the surrounding landscapes. Visitors can hike to the volcano's summit, passing through lava fields and lush vegetation, and witness the mesmerizing sight of molten lava glowing against the night sky

Rio Dulce

Rio Dulce, or "Sweet River," is a scenic waterway flowing through the eastern region of Guatemala, surrounded by dense tropical rainforests and limestone cliffs. Visitors can take boat tours along the river, passing through narrow canyons, spotting wildlife such as howler monkeys and tropical birds

Antigua Guatemala

Antigua Guatemala, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a charming colonial city nestled in the central highlands of Guatemala, surrounded by three towering volcanoes: Agua, Fuego, and Acatenango. Visitors can explore the city's cobblestone streets, colonial architecture, and vibrant markets

Quetzaltenango (Xela)

Quetzaltenango, commonly known as Xela, is Guatemala's second-largest city, situated in the western highlands surrounded by volcanic peaks and lush valleys. Visitors can explore the city's historic center, visit indigenous markets

Lake Petén Itzá

Lake Petén Itzá, located in the northern region of Guatemala near the town of Flores, is a picturesque lake surrounded by dense tropical rainforests and ancient Mayan ruins. Visitors can take boat tours on the lake, swim in its refreshing waters
