GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Croatia

Plitvice Lakes National Park

Plitvice Lakes National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning turquoise lakes, cascading waterfalls, and lush greenery. Visitors can explore wooden walkways and hiking trails that wind through the park's pristine landscapes

Krka National Park

Krka National Park, located along the Krka River in central Dalmatia, is famous for its series of seven picturesque waterfalls and crystal-clear pools. Visitors can swim in the refreshing waters, take boat tours along the river, and hike through scenic trails that lead to viewpoints overlooking the cascades

Paklenica National Park

Paklenica National Park, situated in the Velebit mountain range along the Adriatic coast, is a paradise for hikers, climbers, and outdoor adventurers. Visitors can explore rugged canyons, towering limestone cliffs, and dense beech forests, while hiking along well-marked trails that lead to panoramic viewpoints and hidden caves.

Kornati National Park

Kornati National Park is an archipelago of over 100 uninhabited islands and islets scattered along the Adriatic coast of Croatia. Visitors can take boat tours to explore the park's rugged coastlines, secluded coves, and crystal-clear waters, ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and diving

Brijuni National Park

Brijuni National Park, located on the Brijuni Islands off the Istrian Peninsula, is known for its scenic landscapes, archaeological sites, and diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the islands on guided tours, visit ancient Roman ruins and medieval fortresses

Mljet National Park

Mljet National Park, located on the island of Mljet in the southern Adriatic, is renowned for its pristine forests, tranquil lakes, and ancient monastic sites. Visitors can hike or bike through pine-scented trails, kayak on the emerald-green waters of the Great and Small Lakes

Risnjak National Park

Risnjak National Park, situated in the Gorski Kotar region of Croatia, is a mountainous wilderness known for its diverse landscapes, alpine meadows, and dense beech forests. Visitors can hike to the summit of Risnjak Mountain, enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding peaks and valleys

Croatian Islands

Croatia's numerous islands, including Hvar, Vis, and Brač, offer stunning natural landscapes, secluded beaches, and charming coastal towns. Visitors can explore picturesque harbors, swim in crystal-clear waters, and hike along scenic trails that lead to panoramic viewpoints.
