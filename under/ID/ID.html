GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Indonesia

Baliem Valley, Papua

Baliem Valley is a picturesque highland valley located in the heart of Papua. Surrounded by towering mountains, lush forests, and terraced rice fields, this remote valley is home to indigenous tribes and offers stunning views of pristine landscapes. Visitors can trek through the valley, interact with local tribes, and immerse themselves in the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Komodo National Park, East Nusa Tenggara

Komodo National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the famous Komodo dragons, the world's largest lizards. The park comprises several islands with diverse ecosystems, including rugged hills, savannah, and coral reefs. Visitors can hike on Rinca and Komodo islands, snorkel or dive in crystal-clear waters, and witness the unique wildlife, including Komodo dragons, manta rays, and colorful reef fish.

Borobudur Temple, Central Java

Borobudur Temple is the largest Buddhist temple complex in the world and one of Indonesia's most iconic landmarks. Located in Central Java, this UNESCO World Heritage Site dates back to the 9th century and features intricate stone carvings and stupas. Visitors can explore the temple's three tiers, each representing different stages of enlightenment, and admire panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Raja Ampat Islands, West Papua

Raja Ampat Islands are an archipelago located off the northwest tip of Papua. Known for their unparalleled marine biodiversity and pristine coral reefs, these islands are a paradise for divers and snorkelers. Visitors can explore vibrant coral gardens, swim alongside colorful fish and marine life, and relax on secluded beaches surrounded by lush rainforests.

Mount Bromo, East Java

Mount Bromo is an active volcano situated in the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park. Surrounded by a vast sea of sand and otherworldly landscapes, Mount Bromo is one of Indonesia's most iconic natural attractions. Visitors can hike to the crater rim for sunrise views, ride horseback across the volcanic terrain, and marvel at the surreal beauty of the surrounding landscape.

Ubud, Bali

Ubud is a charming town located in the heart of Bali's lush interior. Surrounded by rice paddies, dense forests, and flowing rivers, Ubud is renowned for its serene ambiance and cultural attractions. Visitors can explore ancient temples, attend traditional dance performances, and rejuvenate with yoga and wellness retreats amidst the natural beauty of the surroundings.

Tanjung Puting National Park, Borneo

Tanjung Puting National Park is a protected area located in the Indonesian part of Borneo. Home to diverse ecosystems, including tropical rainforests and peat swamp forests, the park is famous for its population of wild orangutans. Visitors can take boat cruises along the park's rivers, trek through the jungle, and observe orangutans in their natural habitat at rehabilitation centers.

Lake Toba, North Sumatra

Lake Toba is a massive volcanic lake nestled in the caldera of a supervolcano in North Sumatra. Surrounded by picturesque mountains and verdant landscapes, Lake Toba is one of the largest and deepest lakes in the world. Visitors can relax in lakeside villages, take boat cruises to Samosir Island, and soak in the tranquil beauty of this natural wonder.
