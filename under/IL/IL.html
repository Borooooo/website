GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Israel

Dead Sea

The Dead Sea, located at the lowest point on Earth, is famous for its hypersaline water and therapeutic mud. Visitors can float effortlessly in the buoyant waters, known for their healing properties and high mineral content.

Makhtesh Ramon

Makhtesh Ramon is a massive geological crater located in the Negev Desert. Formed by erosion over millions of years, the crater features dramatic cliffs, colorful rock formations, and vast open spaces. Visitors can hike along scenic trails, marvel at the crater's unique geology,

Ein Gedi Nature Reserve

Ein Gedi Nature Reserve is an oasis in the Judean Desert, known for its lush vegetation, waterfalls, and wildlife. Visitors can hike through winding trails that lead to freshwater springs and cascading waterfalls, providing a refreshing escape from the desert heat.

Mount Masada

Mount Masada, overlooking the Dead Sea, is an ancient fortress built by King Herod over 2,000 years ago. The site is famous for its stunning views, archaeological remains, and historical significance. Visitors can hike the Snake Path or take a cable car to the summit

Banias Nature Reserve

Banias Nature Reserve, located in the Golan Heights, is home to the Banias Waterfall and the ancient city of Caesarea Philippi. Visitors can hike along shaded trails beside rushing streams, discovering hidden waterfalls and lush vegetation.

Rosh Hanikra

Rosh Hanikra is a coastal grotto located on the Mediterranean Sea near the border with Lebanon. Visitors can descend by cable car into the stunning limestone caves, admiring the turquoise waters and intricate rock formations sculpted by the sea.

Timna Park

Timna Park, located in the southern Arava Desert, is known for its unique geological formations and ancient copper mines. Visitors can explore the park's stunning landscapes, including towering rock formations, natural arches, and colorful sandstone cliffs.

Hula Valley Nature Reserve

Hula Valley Nature Reserve is a wetland area in northern Israel, renowned for its rich biodiversity and birdwatching opportunities. Visitors can explore the reserve's network of trails and observation points, spotting migratory birds such as cranes, storks, and pelicans
