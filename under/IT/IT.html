GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Italy

Amalfi Coast

The Amalfi Coast, located in southern Italy, is renowned for its stunning coastal scenery, with rugged cliffs, colorful villages, and sparkling blue waters. Visitors can drive along the scenic coastal road, stopping to explore picturesque towns like Positano

Cinque Terre

Cinque Terre, meaning "Five Lands," is a series of five picturesque fishing villages nestled along the rugged coastline of the Italian Riviera. The villages, perched on cliffs overlooking the Ligurian Sea, are connected by hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the coastline and vineyard-covered hillsides.

Lake Como

Lake Como, located in northern Italy's Lombardy region, is renowned for its breathtaking beauty and serene landscapes. Surrounded by lush forests, snow-capped mountains, and elegant villas, Lake Como has long been a favorite retreat for artists, writers, and celebrities.

The Dolomites

The Dolomites, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a mountain range in northeastern Italy known for their dramatic peaks, rugged terrain, and scenic beauty. Visitors can hike along picturesque trails, admire stunning alpine vistas, and explore charming mountain villages like Cortina d'Ampezzo and Ortisei.

Val d'Orcia

The Val d'Orcia, located in the heart of Tuscany, is a picturesque valley known for its rolling hills, cypress-lined roads, and idyllic landscapes. The region is famous for its charming medieval villages, such as Pienza, Montalcino, and Montepulciano, as well as its vineyards and olive groves.

The Ampezzo Valley

The Ampezzo Valley, located in the Dolomites of northern Italy, is a stunning alpine valley known for its natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities. The valley is home to the charming resort town of Cortina d'Ampezzo, surrounded by soaring peaks, lush forests, and crystal-clear lakes

Mount Etna

Mount Etna, located on the island of Sicily, is one of the most active volcanoes in the world and Europe's tallest volcano. Its lunar landscapes, volcanic craters, and lava fields make it a unique and mesmerizing destination for visitors. Visitors can take guided tours to explore the volcano's rugged terrain, ride cable cars to its summit, and even hike along its lava-strewn slopes for an unforgettable experience.

Apulia Coastlin

The Apulia coastline, located in southern Italy's Apulia region, is known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and picturesque seaside towns. Visitors can explore charming fishing villages like Polignano a Mare and Monopoli, with their whitewashed houses perched on cliffs overlooking the sea
