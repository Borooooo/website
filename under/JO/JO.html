GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Jordania

Petra

Petra, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an ancient city carved into the red sandstone cliffs of southern Jordan. Known as the "Rose City" due to the color of its rock, Petra is famous for its impressive architecture, including the iconic Treasury and the Monastery.

Wadi Rum

Wadi Rum, also known as the "Valley of the Moon," is a stunning desert landscape of sandstone mountains and vast, open plains. Located in southern Jordan, it is home to Bedouin tribes who have inhabited the area for centuries.

Dead Sea

The Dead Sea, located along the border between Jordan and Israel, is the lowest point on Earth and one of the saltiest bodies of water in the world. Its mineral-rich waters and mud are renowned for their therapeutic properties, attracting visitors seeking relaxation and rejuvenation.

Dana Biosphere Reserve

Dana Biosphere Reserve, located in southern Jordan, is the largest nature reserve in the country and home to a diverse range of ecosystems and wildlife. It encompasses rugged mountains, deep canyons, and desert plains, offering opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and eco-tourism.

Jerash

Jerash, located in northern Jordan, is one of the best-preserved Roman cities in the world and a testament to the region's rich history and cultural heritage. The ancient city boasts well-preserved ruins, including colonnaded streets, temples, and theaters, dating back to the Roman period

Ma'in

Ma'in Hot Springs, located near the Dead Sea in western Jordan, are a series of natural hot springs renowned for their therapeutic properties and stunning natural surroundings. Fed by thermal waterfalls cascading down rocky cliffs, the springs offer a relaxing and rejuvenating experience for visitors seeking relief from stress and fatigue

Mount Nebo

Mount Nebo, located near the town of Madaba in western Jordan, is a sacred mountain mentioned in the Bible as the place where Moses saw the Promised Land before he died. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the Jordan Valley, the Dead Sea, and the distant hills of Jerusalem from the summit of Mount Nebo

Azraq

Azraq Wetland Reserve, located in eastern Jordan, is a unique oasis in the heart of the desert and an important habitat for migratory birds and wildlife. The reserve is home to natural springs, seasonal lakes, and marshes, providing a haven for birds such as herons, eagles, and flamingos.
