GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Japan

Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji, Japan's highest peak, is an iconic symbol of the country and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its symmetrical cone shape and snow-capped summit attract climbers and photographers from around the world. Visitors can hike trails around the mountain

Shirakawa-go

Shirakawa-go is a picturesque mountain village located in the remote Gifu Prefecture. It is famous for its traditional thatched-roof houses, known as gassho-zukuri, which are surrounded by lush greenery and rice paddies. Visitors can explore the village, learn about its rural lifestyle and agricultural traditions

Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park

Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park, located in Hiroshima City, is a poignant reminder of the atomic bombing that devastated the city during World War II. The park is home to the Atomic Bomb Dome, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the only building left standing near the bomb's hypocenter

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, located on the outskirts of Kyoto, is a serene bamboo forest that is both beautiful and ethereal. The towering bamboo stalks sway gently in the breeze, creating a mesmerizing atmosphere that transports visitors to another world.

Nikko National Park

Nikko National Park, located in Tochigi Prefecture, is a scenic area known for its lush forests, picturesque waterfalls, and sacred shrines and temples. The park is home to the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Toshogu Shrine, a lavishly decorated mausoleum dedicated to Tokugawa Ieyasu, the founder of the Tokugawa shogunate.

Hokkaido

Hokkaido, Japan's northernmost island, is a vast and diverse region known for its natural beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities. It is home to rugged coastlines, volcanic mountains, and pristine national parks such as Shikotsu-Toya National Park and Daisetsuzan National Park.

Ogasawara Islands

The Ogasawara Islands, also known as the Bonin Islands, are a remote archipelago located south of Tokyo. Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the islands are known for their unique ecosystems and abundant marine life, including dolphins, sea turtles, and coral reefs.

Yakushima

Yakushima is a subtropical island located off the southern coast of Kyushu. It is famous for its ancient cedar forests, some of which are over 1,000 years old, and are designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can hike through lush moss-covered forests, visit the famous Jomon Sugi cedar tree, and marvel at the island's stunning waterfalls and natural hot springs.
