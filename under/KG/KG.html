A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Kyrgyzstan

Issyk-Kul Lake

Issyk-Kul Lake is one of the largest alpine lakes in the world and a breathtaking natural wonder in Kyrgyzstan. Surrounded by snow-capped mountains and lush green valleys, the lake's turquoise waters offer opportunities for swimming, boating, and sunbathing on its sandy beaches

Ala-Archa Gorge

Ala-Archa Gorge, located near the capital city of Bishkek, is a stunning natural park known for its rugged mountains, rushing rivers, and diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can hike along scenic trails, marvel at panoramic views of the surrounding peaks, and picnic beside crystal-clear mountain streams.

Son-Kul Lake

Son-Kul Lake is a pristine alpine lake nestled high in the Tien Shan mountains of central Kyrgyzstan. Surrounded by rolling grasslands and nomadic yurt camps, the lake offers a serene and picturesque setting for visitors to immerse themselves in Kyrgyz nomadic culture.

Jeti-Oguz Valley

Jeti-Oguz Valley, located in the Issyk-Kul region, is known for its distinctive red sandstone cliffs, which resemble seven bulls lying down (the name translates to "Seven Bulls"). The valley is a popular destination for hiking, horseback riding, and camping, with scenic trails leading to waterfalls, hot springs, and panoramic viewpoints.

Sary-Chelek

Sary-Chelek Biosphere Reserve is a pristine wilderness area located in the western part of Kyrgyzstan, known for its diverse ecosystems and stunning natural beauty. The reserve is home to Sary-Chelek Lake, a tranquil alpine lake surrounded by forested mountains and rare flora and fauna.

Tash Rabat

Tash Rabat Caravanserai is a historic stone caravanserai located in the remote mountains of southeastern Kyrgyzstan. Dating back to the 15th century, it served as a resting place for merchants and travelers along the ancient Silk Road

Ala-Kul Lake

Ala-Kul Lake is a stunning glacial lake nestled high in the Terskey Alatau mountain range of eastern Kyrgyzstan. Accessible via a challenging trekking route, the lake offers breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks, alpine meadows, and cascading waterfalls.

Chatyr-Kul Lake

Chatyr-Kul Lake is a remote and pristine alpine lake located in the Pamir Mountains of southeastern Kyrgyzstan, near the border with China. Situated at an altitude of over 3,500 meters above sea level, the lake is surrounded by snow-capped peaks and vast grasslands inhabited by nomadic herders.
