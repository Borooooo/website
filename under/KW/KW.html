GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Kuwait

Kuwait Towers

The Kuwait Towers are iconic architectural landmarks in Kuwait City, offering panoramic views of the city skyline and the Arabian Gulf. Visitors can ascend the towers to enjoy breathtaking vistas of the surrounding landscape, including the shimmering waters of the Gulf and the bustling city below.

Al Shaheed

Al Shaheed Park is a green oasis in the heart of Kuwait City, featuring lush gardens, scenic walking paths, and tranquil lakes. Visitors can escape the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse themselves in nature, enjoying picnics, leisurely strolls, and outdoor concerts in the park's peaceful surroundings.

Failaka Island

Failaka Island is a historic island located off the coast of Kuwait City, known for its archaeological sites, sandy beaches, and scenic landscapes. Visitors can explore ancient ruins dating back to the Mesopotamian era, including temples, tombs, and fortified settlements, as well as enjoy swimming, snorkeling, and water sports along the island's coastline.

Scientific Center

The Scientific Center is a popular educational and recreational complex in Kuwait City, featuring an aquarium, a discovery center, and an IMAX theater. Visitors can learn about marine life native to the Arabian Gulf, explore interactive exhibits on science and technology, and watch educational films on a giant screen.

Al Kout Beach

Al Kout Beach is a pristine stretch of coastline located in the southern part of Kuwait, offering soft white sands, clear turquoise waters, and stunning views of the Gulf. Visitors can relax on the beach, swim in the calm waters

Khiran Resort

Khiran Resort is a luxury beach resort located along the southern coast of Kuwait, offering a tranquil retreat from the city. Visitors can stay in luxurious villas or beachfront chalets, relax on private beaches, and enjoy amenities such as swimming pools, spa treatments, and gourmet dining options.

Al Jahra Nature Reserve

Al Jahra Nature Reserve is a protected area located northwest of Kuwait City, encompassing desert landscapes, wetlands, and wildlife habitats. Visitors can explore hiking trails, observe native flora and fauna, and learn about conservation efforts to protect Kuwait's natural heritage.

Kuwait Zoo

Kuwait Zoo is a zoological park located in Omariya, Kuwait City, housing a diverse collection of animals from around the world. Visitors can see exotic species such as lions, tigers, giraffes, and elephants, as well as native wildlife native to the Arabian Peninsula.
