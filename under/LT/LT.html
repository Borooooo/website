A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Lithuania

Curonian Spit (Kuršių Nerija)

Curonian Spit is a unique sand dune peninsula stretching along the Baltic Sea coast. This UNESCO World Heritage Site features pristine beaches, shifting sand dunes, and dense pine forests. Visitors can explore the quaint fishing villages, climb the Parnidis Dune for panoramic views, and discover the rich biodiversity of the Curonian Spit National Park.

Trakai Historical National Park

Trakai Historical National Park is renowned for its picturesque lakes and medieval castles. Located just outside Vilnius, the capital city, the park encompasses Trakai Island Castle, a stunning red-brick fortress set amidst tranquil waters. Visitors can take boat rides on Lake Galvė, hike through scenic trails, and explore the cultural heritage of the region.

Žemaitija National Park

Žemaitija National Park is a haven of natural beauty located in western Lithuania. The park is characterized by serene lakes, verdant forests, and rolling hills. Visitors can hike or cycle along picturesque trails, go birdwatching in the wetlands, and enjoy recreational activities such as boating, fishing, and camping.

Aukštaitija National Park

Aukštaitija National Park is the oldest national park in Lithuania, known for its pristine wilderness and tranquil lakes. The park features dense forests, meandering rivers, and over 120 lakes, including Lake Lūšiai, the deepest lake in Lithuania. Visitors can explore ancient ethnographic villages, go canoeing or kayaking, and immerse themselves in the unspoiled natural surroundings.

Dzūkija National Park

Dzūkija National Park is a vast forested area in southeastern Lithuania, characterized by its tranquil landscapes and abundant wildlife. The park is home to old-growth forests, crystal-clear rivers, and unique ecosystems. Visitors can hike through the ancient woodlands, spot rare species of plants and animals, and experience the traditional way of life in the Dzūkija region.

Hill of Crosses (Kryžių Kalnas)

The Hill of Crosses is a sacred pilgrimage site located near the city of Šiauliai. This unique hill is adorned with thousands of crosses, symbolizing faith, hope, and resilience. Visitors can stroll through the hillside covered with crosses of all shapes and sizes, reflecting Lithuania's enduring religious traditions and cultural identity.

Nemunas Delta Regional Park

Nemunas Delta Regional Park is a tranquil wetland area where the Nemunas River meets the Curonian Lagoon. The park is renowned for its rich biodiversity, including migratory birds, rare plant species, and diverse habitats. Visitors can explore the network of waterways by boat, observe wildlife in their natural habitat, and enjoy the peaceful ambiance of the delta landscape.

Žuvintas

Žuvintas Biosphere Reserve is a protected area in southern Lithuania, known for its unique peat bog ecosystem and important bird habitats. The reserve is home to the Žuvintas Lake, one of the largest raised bogs in Europe, and serves as a vital breeding ground for numerous bird species, including the rare aquatic warbler. Visitors can explore the reserve on wooden boardwalks, observe birdlife from observation towers, and learn about conservation efforts in the area.
