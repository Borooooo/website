A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of North Macedonia

Mavrovo National Park

Mavrovo National Park is the largest national park in North Macedonia, known for its stunning mountain landscapes, pristine lakes, and diverse wildlife. The park is home to the picturesque Mavrovo Lake, surrounded by dense forests and majestic peaks. Visitors can enjoy hiking, skiing, and wildlife watching in this scenic wilderness area.

Matka Canyon

Matka Canyon is a breathtaking natural wonder located just outside the capital city of Skopje. Carved by the Treska River, the canyon offers stunning scenery with towering cliffs, crystal-clear waters, and lush vegetation. Visitors can explore numerous caves, hike along scenic trails, and take boat trips to discover hidden caves and waterfalls.

Ohrid Lake

Ohrid Lake is one of Europe's oldest and deepest lakes, renowned for its crystal-clear waters, sandy beaches, and rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by picturesque mountains and charming towns, the lake is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination.

Galichica National Park

Galichica National Park is situated on the eponymous Galichica Peninsula between Ohrid and Prespa Lakes, offering breathtaking views of both lakes and their surrounding landscapes. The park is characterized by diverse ecosystems, including forests, meadows, and alpine pastures.

Pelister National Park

Pelister National Park is located in the southwestern part of North Macedonia, known for its rugged mountain terrain, dense forests, and alpine meadows. The park is home to Mount Pelister, the third-highest peak in the country, as well as the rare Molika pine tree and endemic wildlife species

Galicica Mountain

Galicica Mountain is a stunning natural landmark situated between Ohrid and Prespa Lakes, offering panoramic views of both lakes and their surrounding areas. The mountain is known for its rich biodiversity, including rare plant species and diverse wildlife.

Treska Gorge

The Treska River Gorge is a picturesque canyon located near Matka, just a short drive from Skopje. Carved by the Treska River, the gorge features dramatic cliffs, cascading waterfalls, and lush vegetation. Visitors can hike along the riverbank, take boat trips through the canyon, and explore ancient monasteries and caves hidden within the rugged landscape.

Markovi Kuli

Markovi Kuli is a historic fortress located near the town of Prilep, perched atop a rocky hill with commanding views of the surrounding countryside. The fortress dates back to the medieval period and is known for its impressive stone towers and defensive walls.
