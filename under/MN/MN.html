A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Mongolia

Gobi Desert

The Gobi Desert is one of the largest and most iconic deserts in the world, spanning the southern part of Mongolia. It is characterized by vast expanses of sand dunes, rocky outcrops, and unique desert flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the desert on camel or horseback, marvel at the flaming cliffs of Bayanzag, and discover ancient dinosaur fossils in the Flaming Cliffs.

Khövsgöl Lake

Khövsgöl Lake, often referred to as the "Blue Pearl of Mongolia," is a stunning alpine lake located in the northern part of the country. Surrounded by forested mountains and lush meadows, the lake is known for its crystal-clear waters and serene beauty.

Orkhon Valley

The Orkhon Valley is a picturesque river valley located in central Mongolia, known for its scenic beauty and historical significance. It is home to the Orkhon Waterfall, one of the tallest waterfalls in Mongolia, as well as ancient archaeological sites and nomadic herding communities.

Terelj National Park

Terelj National Park is a popular destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, located just a short drive from the capital city of Ulaanbaatar. The park is known for its stunning rock formations, including the famous Turtle Rock, as well as lush valleys, meandering rivers, and alpine meadows.

Khustain Nuruu

Khustain Nuruu National Park is a protected area located in central Mongolia, famous for its efforts to conserve the endangered Przewalski's horse, the last remaining wild horse species in the world. The park is characterized by rolling hills, grassy steppes, and diverse wildlife, including deer, gazelles, and eagles. Visitors can observe the wild horses in their natural habitat

Yolyn Am

Yolyn Am, also known as the Valley of the Eagles, is a narrow gorge located within the Gobi Gurvansaikhan National Park in southern Mongolia. It is famous for its towering cliffs, ice formations, and rich birdlife, including bearded vultures and golden eagles.

Khongoryn Els

Khongoryn Els, also known as the Singing Sands, is a vast sand dune system located in the Gobi Desert of southern Mongolia. The dunes can reach heights of up to 300 meters and stretch for over 180 kilometers across the desert landscape.

Khorgo-Terkhiin

Khorgo-Terkhiin Tsagaan Nuur National Park is a protected area located in the Arkhangai Province of central Mongolia, known for its volcanic landscapes, freshwater lake, and diverse wildlife. The park is home to the extinct Khorgo volcano, with its crater lake and lava fields, as well as the pristine Terkhiin Tsagaan Nuur (White Lake).
