GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Malta

Blue Grotto

The Blue Grotto is a series of picturesque sea caves located on the southern coast of Malta, near the village of Żurrieq. Known for its stunning azure waters and dramatic limestone cliffs, the Blue Grotto is a popular destination for boat tours and snorkeling excursions.

Dwejra Bay

Dwejra Bay is a scenic coastal area located on the western coast of the Maltese island of Gozo. It is famous for its dramatic rock formations, including the iconic Azure Window (formerly), which tragically collapsed in 2017, and the Inland Sea, a natural lagoon connected to the sea by a narrow tunnel.

Marsaxlokk Bay

Marsaxlokk Bay is a charming fishing village located on the southeastern coast of Malta, known for its colorful traditional fishing boats, called Luzzus, and bustling fish market. The bay offers picturesque views of the Mediterranean Sea, with its clear turquoise waters and scenic coastline.

Għadira Bay

Għadira Bay, also known as Mellieħa Bay, is the largest sandy beach in Malta, situated on the northern coast of the main island. With its golden sands and shallow turquoise waters, Għadira Bay is a popular destination for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Gozo

Gozo, Malta's sister island, offers stunning coastal walks along its rugged cliffs and picturesque bays. Visitors can explore scenic hiking trails, such as the Victoria Lines Trail, which offers panoramic views of the island's coastline and countryside. Along the way, hikers can discover hidden coves, ancient watchtowers, and charming fishing villages, immersing themselves in Gozo's natural beauty and rich history.

Marsaskala Rock

Marsaskala Rock Gardens is a scenic coastal area located on the southeastern coast of Malta, near the town of Marsaskala. The gardens feature a series of terraced limestone cliffs, dotted with native plants and shrubs, offering stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.

Marsalforn Bay

Marsalforn Bay is a picturesque seaside village located on the northern coast of Gozo, known for its sandy beaches, clear waters, and vibrant waterfront promenade. The bay offers excellent swimming and snorkeling opportunities, with its calm and sheltered waters

Buskett Gardens

Buskett Gardens is a lush green oasis located in the central part of Malta, near the historic village of Rabat. The gardens feature a variety of native and exotic trees, shrubs, and flowers, as well as winding pathways and tranquil picnic areas
