GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Mexico

Cenotes of Yucatán Peninsula

The Yucatán Peninsula is dotted with thousands of cenotes, natural sinkholes formed by the collapse of limestone bedrock. These cenotes are filled with crystal-clear groundwater, creating stunning turquoise pools perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and diving.

Copper Canyon (Barrancas del Cobre)

Copper Canyon, located in the northern state of Chihuahua, is a breathtaking natural wonder often referred to as Mexico's Grand Canyon. Spanning over 25,000 square miles, it is larger and deeper than the Grand Canyon in the United States. Visitors can explore the canyon by hiking, biking, or taking the scenic Chepe train ride, which offers spectacular views of the rugged terrain and towering cliffs.

Sumidero Canyon (Cañón del Sumidero)

Sumidero Canyon, located in the southern state of Chiapas, is a dramatic geological formation carved by the Grijalva River over millions of years. Towering cliffs rise over 1,000 meters above the river, creating a stunning natural spectacle.

Hierve el Agua

Hierve el Agua, located in the state of Oaxaca, is a unique natural attraction known for its petrified waterfalls and mineral-rich rock formations. These "frozen waterfalls" are created by the buildup of calcium carbonate deposits over thousands of years, forming cascading terraces that resemble cascades of flowing water

Sian Ka'an Biosphere Reserve

Sian Ka'an, located on the Caribbean coast of the Yucatán Peninsula, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Mexico's largest protected areas. It encompasses diverse ecosystems, including tropical forests, mangroves, wetlands, and barrier reefs, teeming with wildlife such as jaguars, manatees, and sea turtles.

Monarch Butterfly Biosphere

The Monarch Butterfly Biosphere Reserve, located in the central states of Michoacán and México, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a critical habitat for millions of monarch butterflies during their annual migration from North America. Visitors can witness the breathtaking spectacle of millions of butterflies filling the sky, clustering on trees, and resting in the forest canopy.

Cabo San Lucas

El Arco is a stunning natural rock formation located at the southern tip of the Baja California Peninsula, near the resort town of Cabo San Lucas. This iconic landmark consists of a towering sea arch carved by the ocean waves over centuries, creating a picturesque backdrop against the azure waters of the Pacific Ocean

Baja California

The waters off the coast of Baja California are a prime destination for whale watching, offering opportunities to see majestic marine mammals such as gray whales, humpback whales, and blue whales. During the winter months, thousands of gray whales migrate from Alaska to the warm waters of Baja California to breed and give birth, providing unforgettable encounters for visitors.
