GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Norway

Geirangerfjord

Geirangerfjord is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the western part of Norway, known for its breathtaking beauty and dramatic landscapes. Carved by glaciers over millions of years, the fjord features steep, towering cliffs, cascading waterfalls, and crystal-clear waters. Visitors can explore the fjord on scenic boat cruises, kayak tours, or hiking trails, experiencing the awe-inspiring natural wonders of this iconic Norwegian landmark.

Lofoten Islands

The Lofoten Islands are an archipelago situated above the Arctic Circle in northern Norway, renowned for their stunning scenery and picturesque fishing villages. The islands boast majestic mountains, pristine beaches, and vibrant fishing communities, offering opportunities for hiking, fishing, and wildlife watching.

Preikestolen (Pulpit Rock)

Preikestolen, also known as Pulpit Rock, is a spectacular cliff formation overlooking the Lysefjord in southwestern Norway. Rising 604 meters above the fjord, it offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and crystal-clear waters below. Visitors can embark on a scenic hike to the top of Preikestolen, following well-marked trails through rocky terrain and lush forests

Norwegian Fjords

Norway is famous for its stunning fjords, including Hardangerfjord, Sognefjord, and Nærøyfjord, among others. These majestic waterways are characterized by steep cliffs, deep blue waters, and lush greenery, creating some of the most picturesque landscapes in the world.

Trolltunga (Troll's Tongue)

Trolltunga is a dramatic rock formation situated above the Ringedalsvatnet lake in southwestern Norway, resembling a giant's tongue jutting out over the valley below. It is one of the most famous hiking destinations in Norway, offering unparalleled views of the surrounding mountains and fjords.

Jotunheimen

Jotunheimen National Park is Norway's premier alpine wilderness area, located in the heart of the country's mountainous region. It is home to some of the highest peaks in Scandinavia, including Galdhøpiggen, Norway's highest mountain. The park boasts rugged landscapes, glacial valleys, and pristine lakes, making it a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Fjærland

Fjærland Glacier Museum is an award-winning museum located in the village of Fjærland, dedicated to the history and science of glaciers. Nearby, the Jostedalsbreen Glacier is the largest glacier in continental Europe, covering vast expanses of rugged terrain in western Norway.

Vøringsfossen Waterfall

Vøringsfossen is one of Norway's most famous waterfalls, located in the Hardangervidda plateau in central Norway. It plunges 182 meters into the Måbødalen valley below, creating a mesmerizing display of cascading water and mist. Visitors can admire the waterfall from viewing platforms perched on the edge of the gorge or hike along trails that offer different vantage points
