GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Nepal

Sagarmatha National Park

Sagarmatha National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Himalayas of eastern Nepal, home to the world's highest peak, Mount Everest. The park features breathtaking landscapes of towering peaks, glaciers, and alpine meadows, as well as diverse flora and fauna, including endangered species such as the snow leopard and red panda.

Annapurna Conservation Area

The Annapurna Conservation Area is a vast protected area in central Nepal, encompassing the Annapurna mountain range and surrounding valleys. It is renowned for its diverse terrain, ranging from subtropical forests to high alpine meadows, as well as panoramic views of snow-capped peaks such as Annapurna and Machapuchare.

Pokhara Valley

Pokhara Valley is a picturesque destination nestled in the foothills of the Annapurna range, known for its tranquil lakes, verdant forests, and stunning mountain views. The city of Pokhara is the gateway to many popular trekking routes, including the Annapurna Circuit and Ghorepani Poon Hill trek. Visitors can relax by Phewa Lake, explore the vibrant streets of Lakeside

Chitwan National Park

Chitwan National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Terai region of southern Nepal, renowned for its rich biodiversity and diverse ecosystems. The park is home to endangered species such as the Bengal tiger, one-horned rhinoceros, and Asian elephant, as well as hundreds of bird species and reptiles. Visitors can enjoy jungle safaris, elephant rides, and canoe trips along the Rapti River

Langtang National Park

Langtang National Park is a protected area in the central Himalayas of Nepal, known for its pristine alpine landscapes, dense forests, and sacred lakes. The park is home to the Tamang and Sherpa ethnic communities, who have preserved their traditional way of life for generations. Visitors can trek through remote villages, lush rhododendron forests, and high mountain passes

Rara Lake

Rara Lake is the largest lake in Nepal, situated in Rara National Park in the remote Karnali region of western Nepal. The lake is renowned for its crystal-clear waters, surrounded by pristine forests and snow-capped peaks reflected in its tranquil surface

Gosaikunda Lake

Gosaikunda Lake is a sacred alpine lake located in the Langtang National Park, revered by Hindus and Buddhists alike for its religious significance. The lake is surrounded by rugged mountains and glaciers, with stunning views of the Langtang Himalayas in the distance. Visitors can trek to Gosaikunda Lake from Dhunche or Syabrubesi, passing through lush forests

Mustang

Mustang, also known as the "Forbidden Kingdom," is a remote region in northern Nepal bordering Tibet, characterized by its arid landscapes, ancient Buddhist monasteries, and traditional Tibetan culture. The region is home to the walled city of Lo Manthang, a UNESCO World Heritage Site
