A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of New Zeland

Fiordland National Park

Fiordland National Park, located in the southwestern corner of the South Island, is renowned for its stunning fjords, rugged mountains, and ancient rainforests. Milford Sound and Doubtful Sound are two of the park's most famous attractions, offering breathtaking scenery of towering cliffs, cascading waterfalls, and crystal-clear waters.

Tongariro National Park

Tongariro National Park, located in the central North Island, is New Zealand's oldest national park and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is known for its dramatic volcanic landscapes, including the iconic peaks of Mount Tongariro, Mount Ngauruhoe (Mount Doom in "The Lord of the Rings"), and Mount Ruapehu

Abel Tasman

Abel Tasman National Park, situated at the northern tip of the South Island, is renowned for its golden beaches, turquoise waters, and lush coastal forests. The park is home to the Abel Tasman Coast Track, one of New Zealand's Great Walks, which winds its way along sandy shores and rocky headlands, offering panoramic views of the Tasman Sea and surrounding islands.

Lake Tekapo

Lake Tekapo is a stunning alpine lake located in the Mackenzie Basin of the South Island, famous for its vibrant turquoise waters and breathtaking mountain vistas. Nearby, Mount Cook National Park is home to New Zealand's highest peak, Aoraki Mount Cook, as well as numerous glaciers, including the iconic Tasman Glacier.

Coromandel Peninsula

The Coromandel Peninsula, located on the North Island's eastern coast, is known for its pristine beaches, rugged coastline, and lush rainforests. Cathedral Cove and Hot Water Beach are two of the peninsula's most popular attractions, offering opportunities for swimming, snorkeling, and digging natural hot springs in the sand.

Rotorua

Rotorua is a geothermal wonderland located in the central North Island, known for its bubbling mud pools, steaming geysers, and colorful hot springs. Te Puia and Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland are two of the city's most famous geothermal parks, offering guided tours and cultural experiences showcasing Maori culture and traditions.

Milford Track

The Milford Track is one of New Zealand's Great Walks, renowned for its spectacular scenery and pristine wilderness. The track traverses Fiordland National Park, starting at Lake Te Anau and ending at Milford Sound, passing through lush rainforests, alpine valleys, and towering mountains along the way.

Bay of Islands

The Bay of Islands is a stunning maritime playground located in the Northland region of the North Island, known for its turquoise waters, sandy beaches, and picturesque islands. Paihia and Russell are two of the bay's main towns, offering opportunities for sailing, kayaking, and dolphin watching in the crystal-clear waters of the bay.
