GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Peru

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an ancient Inca citadel nestled high in the Andes Mountains, surrounded by lush greenery and towering peaks. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its breathtaking panoramic views, intricate stone ruins, and mysterious history. Visitors can hike the famous Inca Trail or take a scenic train ride to reach Machu Picchu

Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon Rainforest covers a vast expanse of Peru's eastern region, teeming with unparalleled biodiversity and pristine wilderness. Visitors can embark on guided jungle expeditions to explore the dense tropical foliage, spot diverse wildlife such as monkeys, birds, and jaguars, and interact with indigenous communities who call the rainforest home

Colca Canyon

Colca Canyon is one of the deepest canyons in the world, located in the Andes Mountains of southern Peru. It is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, with rugged landscapes, terraced fields, and picturesque villages dotting the canyon walls.

Huascarán National Park

Huascarán National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Cordillera Blanca mountain range of northern Peru. It is home to Peru's highest peak, Huascarán, as well as numerous glaciers, lakes, and alpine ecosystems. Visitors can explore the park's stunning landscapes on trekking expeditions, climbing adventures, or scenic drives, encountering breathtaking vistas of snow-capped peaks

Lake Titicaca

Lake Titicaca is the largest lake in South America and the highest navigable lake in the world, straddling the border between Peru and Bolivia. It is renowned for its crystal-clear waters, floating reed islands, and vibrant indigenous culture.

Nazca Lines

The Nazca Lines are a series of ancient geoglyphs etched into the desert floor of southern Peru, dating back to the Nazca civilization. These mysterious figures and lines, including animals, geometric shapes, and humanoids, can only be fully appreciated from the air.

Paracas National Reserve

Paracas National Reserve is a protected coastal area located on the Paracas Peninsula in southwestern Peru. It is renowned for its stunning desert landscapes, pristine beaches, and abundant wildlife, including sea lions, dolphins, and seabirds. Visitors can explore the reserve on guided tours,

Sacred Valley of the Incas

The Sacred Valley of the Incas is a picturesque valley located near Cusco in the Andean highlands of Peru. It is renowned for its fertile agricultural terraces, quaint villages, and ancient Inca ruins. Visitors can explore archaeological sites such as Pisac, Ollantaytambo, and Moray, learning about the rich cultural heritage of the Incas and marveling at the stunning scenery of the Sacred Valley amidst towering mountains and winding rivers.
