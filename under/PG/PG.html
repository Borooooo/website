A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Papua New Guinea

Kokoda Track

The Kokoda Track is a historic trail that traverses the rugged mountains of Papua New Guinea's Oro Province. This iconic trek offers stunning natural beauty, passing through dense rainforests, remote villages, and challenging terrain. Visitors can hike the track, immersing themselves in the rich history and culture of the region while enjoying breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Mount Wilhelm

Mount Wilhelm is the highest peak in Papua New Guinea, located in the Bismarck Range of the Highlands region. Rising to an elevation of 4,509 meters (14,793 feet), the mountain offers spectacular panoramic views of the surrounding valleys, rivers, and neighboring peaks.

Tufi Dive Resort

Tufi Dive Resort is nestled on the remote northern coast of Papua New Guinea, offering access to some of the most pristine coral reefs in the world. The resort is surrounded by lush rainforest, turquoise waters, and dramatic fjords, providing the perfect backdrop for diving, snorkeling, and other water-based activities.

Sepik River

The Sepik River is one of the longest rivers in Papua New Guinea, winding its way through dense rainforest and traditional villages in the remote Sepik Basin. This mighty waterway is teeming with wildlife, including crocodiles, birds, and freshwater fish, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Visitors can embark on boat tours or canoe expeditions along the Sepik River

Milne Bay Province

Milne Bay Province is located in the southeastern part of Papua New Guinea and is renowned for its stunning marine biodiversity and pristine coral reefs. The province is home to numerous islands, including the famous D'Entrecasteaux Islands and the Trobriand Islands, offering opportunities for diving, snorkeling, and island hopping

Mount Hagen

Mount Hagen is a rugged mountain range located in the Western Highlands Province of Papua New Guinea, offering breathtaking views of lush valleys, dense forests, and towering peaks. The region is known for its vibrant traditional cultures, with colorful festivals, traditional dance performances, and tribal gatherings taking place throughout the year.

Rabaul and the Tavurvur Volcano

Rabaul is a town located on the island of New Britain in Papua New Guinea, known for its stunning natural harbor and the active Tavurvur Volcano. The town offers breathtaking views of the surrounding volcanic landscape, with ash-covered hills, hot springs, and bubbling mud pools. Visitors can explore the town's historical sites

Madang Province

Madang Province is located on the northern coast of Papua New Guinea and is renowned for its picturesque coastline, pristine beaches, and vibrant coral reefs. The province is home to numerous islands, including the stunning Manam and Karkar Islands, offering opportunities for diving, snorkeling, and beachcombing.
