GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Poland

Tatra Mountains

The Tatra Mountains, located in southern Poland, are part of the Carpathian range and offer stunning alpine scenery, with rugged peaks, deep valleys, and crystal-clear lakes. Visitors can explore the Tatra National Park, which is home to diverse flora and fauna, including rare species such as the Tatra chamois and golden eagle.

Białowieża Forest

Białowieża Forest, straddling the border between Poland and Belarus, is one of the last remaining primeval forests in Europe and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This ancient woodland is home to the European bison, as well as wolves, lynx, and rare bird species. Visitors can explore the forest on foot or by bike, following trails that wind through old-growth forests, wetlands, and meadows teeming with biodiversity.

Masurian Lakeland

The Masurian Lakeland is a region of interconnected lakes located in northeastern Poland, renowned for its tranquil waterways, lush forests, and picturesque landscapes. Visitors can explore the region by boat, kayak, or canoe, navigating through a network of rivers and lakes dotted with charming villages and historic castles.

Bieszczady Mountains

The Bieszczady Mountains, located in southeastern Poland, are a remote and rugged range characterized by rolling hills, deep valleys, and pristine wilderness. The region is home to diverse wildlife, including European brown bears, Eurasian lynx, and wild boar. Visitors can explore the Bieszczady National Park, hiking through beech and spruce forests, and enjoying panoramic views from peaks such as Tarnica and Połonina Caryńska.

Wieliczka Salt Mine

The Wieliczka Salt Mine, located near the city of Krakow, is one of the oldest salt mines in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can descend underground to explore a labyrinth of tunnels, chambers, and chapels carved entirely out of salt. The mine's most famous feature is the Chapel of St. Kinga, a stunning underground cathedral adorned with intricate salt sculptures and chandeliers.

Ojców National Park

Ojców National Park, located near Krakow, is Poland's smallest national park but offers incredible natural beauty, with limestone cliffs, scenic valleys, and dense forests. Visitors can explore the park's network of hiking trails, discovering geological formations such as the iconic Hercules' Club and the limestone rock pillars known as "maczuga herkulesa."

Babia Góra

Babia Góra, located in the Beskid Mountains of southern Poland, is the country's highest peak outside of the Tatra range. The mountain is part of the Babia Góra National Park, a protected area known for its diverse ecosystems and unique flora and fauna. Visitors can hike to the summit of Babia Góra, enjoying panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys, and exploring the park's alpine meadows and forests.

Slowinski National Park

Slowinski National Park, located on the Baltic coast, is known for its unique shifting sand dunes, which are constantly reshaped by wind and weather. The park is home to diverse habitats, including coastal forests, wetlands, and lakes, as well as rare species of birds and plants. Visitors can explore the park on foot or by boardwalk, discovering the otherworldly landscape of the shifting dunes and the tranquil beauty of the surrounding coastal area.
