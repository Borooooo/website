GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Portugal

Douro Valley

The Douro Valley, located in northern Portugal, is renowned for its stunning landscapes of terraced vineyards, winding river valleys, and picturesque villages. The region is famous for producing Portugal's prized Port wine, and visitors can explore the vineyards, taste local wines, and enjoy scenic boat cruises along the Douro River.

Peneda-Gerês National Park

Peneda-Gerês National Park, located in the northwest of Portugal, is the country's only national park and offers diverse natural beauty, including rugged mountains, lush forests, and cascading waterfalls. Visitors can hike along scenic trails, swim in crystal-clear mountain streams, and discover ancient Roman ruins and traditional mountain villages.

Algarve Coastline

The Algarve Coastline, in southern Portugal, is famous for its stunning cliffs, golden beaches, and turquoise waters. Visitors can explore picturesque towns such as Lagos, Albufeira, and Faro, relax on beautiful beaches like Praia da Marinha and Praia da Falésia, and enjoy water sports such as surfing, snorkeling, and kayaking.

Sintra-Cascais Natural Park

Sintra-Cascais Natural Park, located near Lisbon, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its lush forests, rugged coastline, and historic monuments. Visitors can explore the enchanting town of Sintra, with its fairytale palaces and gardens, hike along scenic trails in the Sintra Mountains, and discover pristine beaches such as Praia do Guincho.

Ria Formosa Natural Park

Ria Formosa Natural Park, located in the Algarve region, is a unique coastal lagoon system known for its diverse ecosystems, including salt marshes, sand dunes, and barrier islands. Visitors can explore the park by boat, spotting a variety of bird species, including flamingos, herons, and spoonbills, and relax on pristine beaches such as Ilha de Faro and Ilha Deserta.

Serra da Estrela

Serra da Estrela, located in central Portugal, is the country's highest mountain range and offers breathtaking alpine scenery, with rugged peaks, deep valleys, and pristine lakes. Visitors can explore the Serra da Estrela Natural Park, hike to the summit of Torre, the highest peak in Portugal, and enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports in the colder months.

Arrábida

Arrábida Natural Park, located near Lisbon, is a hidden gem known for its dramatic cliffs, secluded beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Visitors can hike along scenic trails, explore ancient convents and fortresses, and relax on pristine beaches such as Praia dos Coelhos and Praia da Figueirinha.

Dona Ana Beach

Dona Ana Beach, located near Lagos in the Algarve, is often considered one of the most beautiful beaches in Portugal, with golden sand, turquoise waters, and towering limestone cliffs. Visitors can relax on the beach, swim in the clear waters, and explore nearby caves and rock formations by boat or kayak.
