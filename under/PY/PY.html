A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Paraguay

Iguazu Falls

While predominantly located in Brazil and Argentina, a portion of the majestic Iguazu Falls can be viewed from Paraguay's side. These iconic waterfalls are among the largest and most impressive in the world, with cascades plunging over towering cliffs amidst lush rainforest.

Ñeembucú Wetlands

The Ñeembucú Wetlands, situated in southwestern Paraguay along the Paraguay River, form a vast and diverse ecosystem of marshes, lagoons, and channels. This region is rich in biodiversity, with numerous bird species, including storks, herons, and ibises, as well as caimans, capybaras, and other wildlife. Visitors can explore the wetlands by boat, observing wildlife and enjoying the tranquility of this pristine natural environment.

Saltos del Monday

Saltos del Monday is a beautiful waterfall located near the city of Ciudad del Este in eastern Paraguay. The waterfall tumbles over a series of rocky cliffs surrounded by lush vegetation, creating a picturesque natural setting. Visitors can hike along trails that offer panoramic views of the falls, relax in shaded picnic areas, and enjoy the peaceful ambiance of this scenic spot.

Ybycuí National Park

Ybycuí National Park, located in the Paraguayan department of Paraguarí, encompasses diverse landscapes, including dense forests, rolling hills, and cascading waterfalls. The park is home to abundant wildlife, including jaguars, tapirs, and howler monkeys, as well as a variety of bird species.

Cerro Corá National Park

Cerro Corá National Park, located in the Amambay department near the border with Brazil, is known for its rugged terrain, deep canyons, and rich biodiversity. The park is home to a variety of plant and animal species, including the endangered jaguarundi and giant anteater. Visitors can explore the park's trails, visit historic sites such as the Cerro Corá Hill, and enjoy birdwatching and wildlife spotting opportunities.

Ypacaraí Lake

Ypacaraí Lake is a scenic lake located in the Central Department of Paraguay, known for its tranquil waters and surrounding natural beauty. The lake offers opportunities for swimming, boating, and fishing, as well as picnicking and camping along its shores. Visitors can enjoy stunning sunsets over the lake and explore nearby towns such as Areguá, known for its pottery and handicrafts.

Ñu Guasú Park

Ñu Guasú Park, located near the capital city of Asunción, is a sprawling natural reserve that offers recreational activities and outdoor experiences. The park features expansive grasslands, forests, and wetlands, as well as walking and biking trails. Visitors can enjoy birdwatching, wildlife spotting, and picnicking amidst the scenic landscapes of this urban oasis.

San Rafael

San Rafael National Park, located in the Itapúa Department in southeastern Paraguay, is a protected area renowned for its diverse ecosystems and stunning landscapes. The park encompasses dense subtropical forests, rolling hills, and the San Rafael Waterfall, one of the tallest waterfalls in Paraguay.
