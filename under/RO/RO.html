GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Romania

Transfăgărășan Road:

Transfăgărășan is a spectacular mountain road that winds through the Făgăraș Mountains in central Romania. Known for its dramatic scenery, hairpin bends, and breathtaking views, this road is often considered one of the most scenic drives in the world. Visitors can enjoy panoramic vistas of rugged peaks, alpine lakes, and dense forests as they traverse this mountain pass.

Danube Delta

The Danube Delta, located in eastern Romania, is the second-largest river delta in Europe and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This vast wetland area is home to a rich diversity of flora and fauna, including numerous bird species, fish, and rare plants. Visitors can explore the delta by boat, navigating through winding channels, marshes, and lagoons while observing wildlife and enjoying the serene natural beauty.

Retezat National Park

Retezat National Park, located in the Carpathian Mountains of western Romania, is renowned for its stunning alpine landscapes, glacial lakes, and diverse ecosystems. The park is home to over 80 glacial lakes, including the picturesque Bucura Lake, as well as pristine forests, meadows, and alpine peaks. Visitors can hike along well-marked trails, summit peaks such as Peleaga, and marvel at the park's natural beauty.

Bicaz Gorge

Bicaz Gorge, also known as Cheile Bicazului, is a dramatic limestone canyon located in the Eastern Carpathians of northeastern Romania. Formed by the Bicaz River cutting through the mountains, the gorge features towering cliffs, rock formations, and narrow passages.

Turda Gorge

Turda Gorge, or Cheile Turzii, is a stunning natural reserve located in central Romania, near the city of Cluj-Napoca. The gorge is characterized by towering limestone cliffs, lush vegetation, and the meandering Turda River. Visitors can hike along trails that wind through the gorge, crossing wooden bridges and passing by caves, waterfalls, and rock formations sculpted by erosion.

Apuseni Mountains

The Apuseni Mountains, located in western Romania, are a picturesque range known for their karst landscapes, underground caves, and traditional villages. The region is dotted with over 400 caves, including the famous Scarisoara Ice Cave, which features a massive ice formation year-round. Visitors can hike through scenic valleys, explore caves, and discover charming rural communities with their unique cultural heritage.

Maramureș

Maramureș is a historic region in northern Romania known for its unspoiled natural beauty, traditional wooden churches, and rustic villages. Visitors can explore picturesque landscapes of rolling hills, lush meadows, and dense forests, as well as visit UNESCO-listed wooden churches and centuries-old wooden houses adorned with intricate carvings.

Peștera Valea Cetății

Peștera Valea Cetății, or Fortress Valley Cave, is a fascinating natural attraction located in the Apuseni Mountains of western Romania. This underground cave system features impressive stalactites, stalagmites, and underground rivers, as well as archaeological discoveries dating back thousands of years
