GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Serbia

Tara National Park

Tara National Park is located in western Serbia and is known for its lush forests, deep gorges, and pristine rivers. Visitors can explore the scenic Drina River Canyon, hike through dense beech and pine forests, and marvel at the stunning views from Banjska Stena viewpoint. The park is also home to diverse wildlife, including brown bears, wolves, and eagles.

Drvengrad (Kustendorf)

Drvengrad, also known as Kustendorf, is a unique ethno-village located in the Zlatibor Mountains. Created by filmmaker Emir Kusturica, this charming village features traditional wooden houses, cobblestone streets, and cultural landmarks. Visitors can experience the authentic atmosphere of rural Serbia, attend film festivals and cultural events, and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Đavolja Varoš (Devil's Town)

Đavolja Varoš is a peculiar rock formation located in southern Serbia, near the town of Kuršumlija. Known as Devil's Town, this natural phenomenon consists of a series of towering stone pillars formed by erosion over millions of years. Visitors can explore the eerie landscape, learn about the local legends surrounding the site, and admire the unique geological formations.

Uvac Canyon

Uvac Canyon is a stunning natural attraction located in southwestern Serbia. Known for its meandering river, dramatic cliffs, and diverse wildlife, the canyon offers breathtaking views and opportunities for outdoor activities. Visitors can take boat tours along the river, hike through the rugged terrain, and spot rare Griffon vultures nesting in the cliffs.

Đerdap National Park

Đerdap National Park is situated along the Danube River in eastern Serbia and is known for its spectacular river gorges and ancient Roman ruins. Visitors can explore the Iron Gate Gorge, one of the largest river gorges in Europe, hike through dense forests, and visit archaeological sites such as the Lepenski Vir prehistoric settlement.

Ovčar-Kablar Gorge

Ovčar-Kablar Gorge is a picturesque canyon located in central Serbia, near the city of Čačak. Surrounded by steep cliffs, dense forests, and meandering rivers, the gorge offers stunning natural scenery and opportunities for outdoor recreation. Visitors can hike along scenic trails, explore Orthodox monasteries nestled in the cliffs, and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Golubac Fortress

Golubac Fortress is a medieval fortress situated on the banks of the Danube River in eastern Serbia. Dating back to the 14th century, the fortress is known for its strategic location and impressive architecture. Visitors can explore the well-preserved ruins, learn about the fortress's history, and enjoy panoramic views of the river and surrounding countryside.

Vlasina Lake

Vlasina Lake is a serene freshwater lake located in southeastern Serbia, near the town of Surdulica. Surrounded by lush meadows, forests, and rolling hills, the lake is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and nature lovers. Visitors can relax on the shores, go birdwatching, and enjoy the tranquility of this pristine natural oasis.
