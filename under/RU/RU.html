GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Russia

Lake Baikal

Lake Baikal, located in Siberia, is the deepest and oldest freshwater lake in the world, known for its stunning blue waters and unique biodiversity. Surrounded by mountain ranges and dense forests, the lake is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a natural wonder of unparalleled beauty. Visitors can explore its shores, hike in the nearby mountains, and experience the tranquility of this pristine natural environment.

Kamchatka Peninsula

The Kamchatka Peninsula, located in Russia's Far East, is a land of breathtaking natural beauty, with its snow-capped volcanoes, hot springs, and rugged coastline. Home to over 160 volcanoes, including the iconic Klyuchevskaya Sopka, the highest volcano in Eurasia, Kamchatka offers unparalleled opportunities for adventure and exploration.

Altai Mountains

The Altai Mountains, located in southern Siberia, are a remote and majestic range known for their pristine landscapes, alpine meadows, and crystal-clear rivers. Designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Altai is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering opportunities for trekking, horseback riding, and rafting amidst breathtaking scenery.

Kizhi Island

Kizhi Island, located in Lake Onega in northwestern Russia, is home to one of the most remarkable open-air museums in the world. The island is famous for its collection of historic wooden churches and buildings, including the iconic Church of the Transfiguration with its 22 onion-domed towers

Kronotsky

Kronotsky Nature Reserve, located on the Kamchatka Peninsula, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers. The reserve is home to diverse ecosystems, including volcanic landscapes, geysers, and pristine wilderness inhabited by brown bears, foxes, and eagles.

Golden Ring of Russia

The Golden Ring is a historic region in northeastern Russia, known for its charming medieval towns, onion-domed churches, and picturesque countryside. The region's historic towns, including Suzdal, Vladimir, and Yaroslavl, are home to well-preserved architectural treasures and UNESCO-listed sites.

Valley of the Geysers

The Valley of the Geysers, located on the Kamchatka Peninsula, is one of the largest geyser fields in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Surrounded by towering mountains and lush forests, the valley is home to numerous hot springs, mud pots, and geysers that erupt with scalding water and steam.

Lena Pillars

Lena Pillars Nature Park, located in eastern Siberia along the Lena River, is known for its stunning rock formations, towering pillars of limestone that rise hundreds of feet above the riverbanks. Designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the park offers breathtaking vistas of these natural wonders, as well as opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife spotting in the surrounding taiga forests.
