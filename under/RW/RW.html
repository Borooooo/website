A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Volcanoes National Park

Volcanoes National Park, located in the northwest of Rwanda, is home to the majestic Virunga Mountains and a population of endangered mountain gorillas. Visitors can embark on guided gorilla trekking expeditions to observe these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat, surrounded by lush rainforest and mist-covered peaks.

Nyungwe Forest National Park

Nyungwe Forest National Park, situated in the southwest of Rwanda, is one of the oldest and most biodiverse rainforests in Africa. The park is a haven for primates, including chimpanzees and colobus monkeys, as well as over 300 bird species.

Lake Kivu

Lake Kivu, located in the western part of Rwanda, is one of Africa's great lakes and a scenic destination known for its clear blue waters and picturesque landscapes. Visitors can relax on sandy beaches, swim in the lake's tranquil waters, and enjoy water sports such as kayaking and sailing. The lake is also famous for its breathtaking sunsets and views of the surrounding mountains.

Akagera National Park

Akagera National Park, situated in the east of Rwanda along the border with Tanzania, is a diverse wilderness area characterized by savannah plains, wetlands, and lakes. Gishwati-Mukura National Park

Gishwati-Mukura National Park, located in the northwest of Rwanda, is a recently established protected area known for its lush montane forests and rich biodiversity. The park is home to chimpanzees, golden monkeys, and other primates, as well as numerous bird species. Visitors can explore the park on guided hikes, discovering waterfalls, scenic viewpoints, and diverse flora and fauna along the way.

Karongi (Kibuye)

Karongi, also known as Kibuye, is a picturesque town located on the shores of Lake Kivu in western Rwanda. Surrounded by lush hills and verdant countryside, Karongi offers stunning views of the lake and opportunities for relaxation and exploration.

Rugezi Marsh

Rugezi Marsh, located in the northern part of Rwanda, is a wetland area renowned for its scenic beauty and rich biodiversity. The marsh is home to numerous bird species, including the rare and endangered shoebill stork, as well as mammals such as antelope and buffalo.

Musaze Caves

Musaze Caves, located near the town of Musanze in northern Rwanda, are a fascinating natural attraction formed by ancient lava flows. The caves feature underground passages, chambers, and rock formations, offering a unique opportunity for exploration and discovery.
