GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Sweden

Stockholm Archipelago

The Stockholm Archipelago consists of over 30,000 islands, islets, and skerries spread across the Baltic Sea. This stunning maritime landscape offers endless opportunities for sailing, kayaking, and island hopping. Visitors can explore charming fishing villages, hike along scenic trails, and enjoy picnics on secluded beaches while soaking in the beauty of the rugged coastline and pristine waters.

Abisko National Park

Abisko National Park, located in Swedish Lapland near the border with Norway, is renowned for its dramatic mountain scenery, deep valleys, and pristine wilderness. The park is home to Sweden's most famous hiking trail, the Kungsleden (King's Trail), which winds through rugged terrain, past crystal-clear lakes, and beneath towering peaks.

Sarek National Park

Sarek National Park, situated in the heart of Swedish Lapland, is one of Europe's last remaining wilderness areas, characterized by rugged mountains, glaciers, and deep valleys. This remote and untouched landscape offers unparalleled opportunities for trekking, mountaineering, and wildlife watching.

Gotland Island

Gotland is Sweden's largest island and a popular destination known for its medieval towns, sandy beaches, and picturesque countryside. Visitors can explore the charming cobblestone streets of Visby, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, wander through ancient ruins and medieval churches, and relax on pristine beaches along the island's coastline. Gotland also boasts unique natural attractions such as the dramatic sea stacks of Raukar.

Vänern Lake

Vänern is Sweden's largest lake and one of Europe's largest freshwater bodies, offering scenic beauty and abundant recreational opportunities. Visitors can cruise along its tranquil waters, exploring islands and coastal towns, or engage in fishing, swimming, and water sports. The lake is surrounded by lush forests, rolling hills, and nature reserves, providing ample opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife spotting.

High Coast/Kvarken Archipelago

The High Coast and Kvarken Archipelago, located along the Gulf of Bothnia in northern Sweden, comprise a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its unique geological features and stunning coastal landscapes. The area is characterized by its post-glacial rebound, which has led to the emergence of a dramatic shoreline with towering cliffs, rocky islands, and picturesque fishing villages.

Lapland Wilderness

Swedish Lapland is a vast and remote region of pristine wilderness, stretching across the northernmost part of the country. Here, visitors can experience the enchanting beauty of the Arctic landscape, with its endless forests, crystal-clear lakes, and snow-capped mountains. The region offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including dog sledding, snowmobiling

Stora Alvaret

Stora Alvaret is a unique limestone plain located on the island of Öland in the Baltic Sea. This expansive natural area is home to a diverse range of plant and animal species, including rare orchids and bird species. Visitors can explore the open landscape on foot or by bike, marveling at the rugged beauty of the limestone formations and enjoying panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and coastline.
