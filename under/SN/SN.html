GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Senegal

Djoudj National Bird Sanctuary

Djoudj National Bird Sanctuary is located in the Senegal River Delta, near the border with Mauritania. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a haven for birdwatchers, with over 400 bird species, including pelicans, flamingos, and herons, making it their home. Visitors can explore the sanctuary by boat, gliding through mangrove forests and wetlands teeming with birdlife.

Lac Rose (Pink Lake)

Lac Rose, or the Pink Lake, is a unique natural wonder located north of Dakar. The lake gets its pink hue from a combination of salt-loving microorganisms and high salt content, creating a stunning landscape that changes color throughout the day. Visitors can take a guided tour around the lake, marveling at its otherworldly beauty and learning about the traditional salt harvesting methods used by local communities.

Niokolo-Koba National Park

Niokolo-Koba National Park is one of the largest and oldest national parks in West Africa, situated in southeastern Senegal. The park is a biodiversity hotspot, home to a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, lions, and chimpanzees. Visitors can embark on safari tours through the park, encountering rare and endangered species in their natural habitat while exploring diverse ecosystems ranging from savannah grasslands to dense forests.

Saloum Delta National Park

Saloum Delta National Park is located in the Saloum Delta, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve known for its rich biodiversity and unique mangrove ecosystems. The park is a sanctuary for migratory birds, marine life, and endangered species such as manatees and sea turtles. Visitors can explore the park by boat, navigating through winding waterways, lush mangrove forests, and tidal channels while observing wildlife and traditional fishing villages along the way.

Fathala Wildlife Reserve

Fathala Wildlife Reserve is a private nature reserve located in the Casamance region of Senegal. The reserve is home to a variety of African wildlife, including giraffes, rhinoceroses, and antelopes, as well as a diverse array of bird species. Visitors can embark on guided safari tours through the reserve, encountering majestic animals in their natural habitat and learning about conservation efforts to protect endangered species.

Nianing Lagoon

Nianing Lagoon is a tranquil coastal lagoon located near the village of Nianing, south of Dakar. The lagoon is known for its calm waters, sandy shores, and scenic surroundings, making it an ideal spot for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports. Visitors can relax on the beach, go snorkeling or kayaking in the lagoon, or simply enjoy the peaceful ambiance and stunning sunsets over the water.

Langue de Barbarie National Park

Langue de Barbarie National Park is situated on a narrow sandy peninsula near the mouth of the Senegal River, offering a unique blend of coastal and desert landscapes. The park is a haven for migratory birds, with thousands of waterfowl flocking to its shores during the winter months. Visitors can explore the park's pristine beaches, sand dunes, and salt marshes, observing birdlife

Bandia Wildlife Reserve

Bandia Wildlife Reserve is a private game reserve located near the seaside resort town of Saly. The reserve is home to a variety of African wildlife, including zebras, giraffes, and crocodiles, as well as over 120 species of birds. Visitors can embark on guided safari tours through the reserve
