A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Chad

Zakouma National Park

Zakouma National Park is located in southeastern Chad and is renowned for its diverse wildlife and pristine landscapes. The park is home to a wide variety of species, including elephants, lions, giraffes, and hundreds of bird species. Visitors can enjoy guided safaris, game drives, and nature walks, experiencing the beauty and tranquility of the African savannah.

Ennedi Plateau

The Ennedi Plateau is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in northeastern Chad, known for its stunning rock formations, towering sandstone pillars, and ancient rock art. The plateau offers breathtaking scenery, with rugged canyons, natural arches, and hidden oases waiting to be explored. Visitors can hike through the labyrinthine canyons, marvel at the ancient rock art, and witness the beauty of the Sahara Desert.

Lake Chad

Lake Chad is a large freshwater lake located in the Sahelian region of Chad, bordered by Chad, Cameroon, Niger, and Nigeria. The lake is a vital ecosystem, supporting diverse flora and fauna, as well as local communities that rely on its resources for fishing and agricultur

Ounianga Lakes

The Ounianga Lakes are a series of stunning desert lakes located in the Ennedi Region of northeastern Chad. These interconnected lakes are known for their turquoise waters, surrounded by towering sand dunes and rocky outcrops. Visitors can hike around the lakes, swim in their pristine waters, and marvel at the stark beauty of the desert landscape.

Guelta d'Archei

Guelta d'Archei is a picturesque oasis located in the Ennedi Region of northeastern Chad, renowned for its dramatic scenery and resident population of critically endangered Saharan crocodiles. The oasis is nestled within a deep canyon, with towering cliffs and palm trees providing shade and shelter

Tibesti Mountains

The Tibesti Mountains are a remote and rugged mountain range located in northern Chad, near the borders with Libya and Niger. The mountains are characterized by their volcanic peaks, deep valleys, and surreal landscapes. Visitors can explore the region on adventurous trekking expeditions, encountering ancient rock art, hot springs, and stunning panoramic views along the way.

Aouk

Aouk National Park is located in the Guéra Region of central Chad and is known for its diverse ecosystems, including savannah, woodland, and wetlands. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including elephants, hippos, and antelopes, as well as over 300 bird species

Manda

Manda National Park is situated in the southwestern part of Chad and is characterized by its lush wetlands, forests, and rivers. The park is home to a rich diversity of flora and fauna, including chimpanzees, buffalo, and rare bird species.
