A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Tunisia

Ichkeul National Park

Ichkeul National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in northern Tunisia, known for its diverse ecosystems and important wetlands. The park is home to Lake Ichkeul, a seasonal freshwater lake surrounded by marshes, forests, and grasslands. It serves as an important stopover for migratory birds, including flamingos and ducks, making it a paradise for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts.

Djerba Island

Djerba Island is the largest island in North Africa, located off the coast of southeastern Tunisia. It is renowned for its stunning beaches, turquoise waters, and rich cultural heritage. Visitors can explore the island's traditional Berber villages, historic mosques, and ancient ruins, or simply relax on its pristine beaches and enjoy the tranquil atmosphere.

Matmata Desert

Matmata Desert is a surreal lunar-like landscape located in southern Tunisia, characterized by its distinctive Berber troglodyte dwellings, known as "troglodyte caves." These underground structures provide shelter from the scorching heat of the desert and offer a unique glimpse into traditional Berber life. Visitors can explore the caves, learn about local customs and traditions, and marvel at the stark beauty of the desert landscape.

Sidi Bouhlel Canyon

Sidi Bouhlel Canyon, also known as Star Wars Canyon, is a breathtaking gorge located near the town of Tozeur in southwestern Tunisia. It gained international fame as a filming location for the Star Wars movie franchise. Visitors can hike through the canyon, marvel at its towering rock formations and natural arches, and imagine themselves in a galaxy far, far away.

Dougga

Dougga is an ancient Roman city located in northern Tunisia, renowned for its well-preserved ruins and stunning hilltop setting. The UNESCO World Heritage Site features impressive monuments, including a theater, temples, and an ancient marketplace, offering insights into Tunisia's rich history and cultural heritage. Visitors can explore the ruins, admire panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, and imagine life in ancient times.

Chott el Jerid

Chott el Jerid is a vast salt lake located in southwestern Tunisia, near the town of Tozeur. The lake is characterized by its shimmering white salt flats, which stretch as far as the eye can see, creating a surreal and otherworldly landscape. Visitors can stop at roadside viewpoints to admire the lake's beauty, take photographs, and learn about its geological significance.

Kerkennah Islands

The Kerkennah Islands are a group of small islands located off the coast of eastern Tunisia, known for their unspoiled beaches, clear waters, and laid-back atmosphere. Visitors can relax on the islands' sandy beaches, swim in the Mediterranean Sea, and explore traditional fishing villages. The islands offer a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of mainland Tunisia.

Tunisian Sahara Desert

The Tunisian Sahara Desert covers much of the country's southern region and is famous for its vast sand dunes, rocky plateaus, and dramatic landscapes. Visitors can embark on camel treks or 4x4 excursions to explore the desert, camp under the stars, and experience the silence and solitude of this remote wilderness
