GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Turkey

Cappadocia

Cappadocia is a unique region in central Turkey known for its surreal landscapes, including fairy chimneys, rock formations, and underground cities. Visitors can take hot air balloon rides over the stunning valleys and rock formations, explore ancient cave dwellings and churches carved into the rock, and hike through the picturesque valleys dotted with vineyards and orchards.

Pamukkale

Pamukkale, meaning "cotton castle" in Turkish, is a natural wonder located in southwestern Turkey. It is famous for its terraces of white travertine terraces filled with thermal waters, creating stunning cascading pools. Visitors can bathe in the warm mineral-rich waters, walk barefoot on the terraces, and explore the ancient ruins of Hierapolis, an ancient Roman spa city built nearby

Oludeniz Beach

Oludeniz Beach, located on the southwest coast of Turkey, is renowned for its crystal-clear turquoise waters and stunning natural surroundings. The beach is framed by dramatic cliffs and lush pine forests, creating a picturesque setting for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports such as paragliding. The nearby Blue Lagoon is a protected nature reserve known for its calm waters and vibrant marine life.

Goremek

Goreme National Park, located in Cappadocia, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its unique rock formations, fairy chimneys, and ancient cave dwellings. Visitors can explore the park on foot or by horseback, marveling at the surreal landscapes and visiting historic rock-cut churches decorated with frescoes dating back to the Byzantine era.

Mount Nemrut

Mount Nemrut, located in southeastern Turkey, is home to a stunning archaeological site and UNESCO World Heritage Site known as the Mount Nemrut National Park. The site features colossal statues and stone reliefs built by King Antiochus I of Commagene in the 1st century BC.

Saklikent Gorge

Saklikent Gorge, located in southwestern Turkey near the town of Fethiye, is one of the deepest and longest canyons in the country. Visitors can hike through the narrow gorge, wading through shallow streams and admiring the towering cliffs and rock formations. During the summer months

Duden Waterfalls

Duden Waterfalls are a series of stunning waterfalls located near the city of Antalya on the Mediterranean coast. The upper Duden Waterfalls cascade over cliffs into a tranquil pool below, while the lower Duden Waterfalls flow directly into the sea.

Lake Van

Lake Van is the largest lake in Turkey and one of the largest alkaline lakes in the world, located in the eastern part of the country. The lake is surrounded by majestic mountains, including Mount Suphan and Mount Nemrut, and is known for its azure-blue waters and volcanic landscapes. Visitors can explore the historic Armenian churches and castles dotting the shoreline
