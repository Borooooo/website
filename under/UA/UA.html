GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Ukraine

Carpathian Mountains

The Carpathian Mountains stretch across western Ukraine, offering breathtaking landscapes of rolling hills, dense forests, and picturesque alpine meadows. Visitors can explore hiking trails that lead to stunning viewpoints, encounter traditional villages nestled among the mountains, and enjoy outdoor activities such as skiing, snowboarding, and horseback riding.

Sofiyivka Park

Sofiyivka Park is a romantic landscape park located in Uman, central Ukraine. Designed in the 19th century, the park features lush gardens, scenic lakes, and winding pathways adorned with classical sculptures and architectural landmarks. Visitors can stroll through the park's picturesque landscapes, admire its unique flora and fauna, and relax in its tranquil atmosphere.

Dnieper River

The Dnieper River is one of the major rivers in Ukraine, flowing through the heart of the country from north to south. Along its banks, visitors can find scenic spots for picnics, fishing, and boating, as well as historic cities and towns with charming waterfronts and architectural landmarks.

Oleshky Sands

Oleshky Sands is one of the largest sand dune systems in Europe, located in the Kherson region of southern Ukraine. This unique natural phenomenon features vast expanses of sandy terrain, sculpted by wind and water into stunning landscapes of dunes and sand ridges.

Sviatohirsk Lavra

Sviatohirsk Lavra is a historic Orthodox monastery located on the banks of the Seversky Donets River in eastern Ukraine. Surrounded by dense forests and scenic cliffs, the monastery complex features beautiful churches, bell towers, and ancient caves carved into the limestone cliffs. Visitors can explore the monastery's architectural treasures, attend religious services, and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

Dniester Canyon

Dniester Canyon is a stunning natural wonder located in western Ukraine, carved by the Dniester River over millions of years. The canyon features towering limestone cliffs, lush forests, and crystal-clear waters, creating a picturesque setting for outdoor activities such as kayaking, rafting, and hiking.

Synevyr Lake

Synevyr Lake is the largest lake in the Carpathian Mountains and one of Ukraine's most beautiful natural attractions. Surrounded by dense forests and towering peaks, the lake's emerald waters reflect the stunning landscapes, creating a serene and picturesque setting. Visitors can hike around the lake, enjoy picnics on its shores, and explore nearby trails that lead to breathtaking viewpoints.

Khortytsia Island

Khortytsia Island is the largest island on the Dnieper River, located near the city of Zaporizhzhia in southern Ukraine. The island is known for its rich history, diverse landscapes, and cultural heritage. Visitors can explore ancient archaeological sites, scenic hiking trails, and traditional Cossack villages, and learn about the island's role in Ukrainian history and culture.
