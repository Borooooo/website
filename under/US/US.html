GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of USA

Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon, located in Arizona, is one of the most iconic natural wonders of the world. Carved by the Colorado River over millions of years, the canyon stretches for miles with its steep cliffs and colorful rock formations. Visitors can hike along its rim trails, take a mule ride to the canyon floor, or raft down the river for a truly immersive experience in this awe-inspiring landscape.

Yellowstone National Park

Yellowstone National Park: Yellowstone National Park, located in Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho, is America's first national park and is renowned for its geothermal wonders and diverse wildlife. Visitors can marvel at the iconic Old Faithful geyser, explore colorful hot springs and bubbling mud pots, and spot bison, elk, wolves, and grizzly bears roaming freely in their natural habitat.

Yosemite National Park

Yosemite National Park, located in California's Sierra Nevada mountains, is famous for its towering granite cliffs, majestic waterfalls, and ancient sequoia trees. Visitors can hike to iconic landmarks such as Half Dome and El Capitan, admire the beauty of Yosemite Falls, and marvel at the giant sequoias in Mariposa Grove.

Great Smoky Mountains National Park

Great Smoky Mountains National Park, straddling the border between North Carolina and Tennessee, is known for its mist-covered mountains, lush forests, and diverse plant and animal life. Visitors can explore scenic drives, hike through old-growth forests, and discover historic log cabins and grist mills nestled within the park's boundaries.

Zion National Park

Zion National Park, located in Utah, is characterized by its towering sandstone cliffs, deep slot canyons, and emerald-green Virgin River. Visitors can hike along the famous Narrows trail, where the canyon walls narrow to just a few feet apart, or explore the stunning landscapes of Angels Landing and the Subway.

Glacier National Park

Glacier National Park, located in Montana, is home to rugged mountains, pristine lakes, and more than 700 miles of hiking trails. Visitors can drive along the scenic Going-to-the-Sun Road, hike to breathtaking vistas such as Grinnell Glacier and Hidden Lake, and spot wildlife such as grizzly bears, moose, and mountain goats.

Hawaii Volcanoes National Park

Hawaii Volcanoes National Park, located on the Big Island of Hawaii, is home to two of the world's most active volcanoes, Kilauea and Mauna Loa. Visitors can witness the power of nature as lava flows into the sea, explore volcanic landscapes such as Thurston Lava Tube and Devastation Trail, and marvel at the glowing lava lake within Halema'uma'u Crater.

Denali National Park

Denali National Park, located in Alaska, is home to North America's tallest peak, Denali (formerly known as Mount McKinley). The park's vast wilderness encompasses tundra, forests, and glaciers, providing habitat for a variety of wildlife, including grizzly bears, wolves, and caribou. Visitors can take guided bus tours or backcountry hikes to explore the park's remote landscapes and experience the true wilderness of Alaska.
