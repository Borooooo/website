A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Urugay

Punta del Este

Punta del Este is a glamorous beach resort town located on Uruguay's southeastern coast. Known for its pristine beaches, scenic coastline, and vibrant nightlife, Punta del Este is a popular destination for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions such as the iconic La Mano sculpture and the picturesque Casapueblo.

Cabo Polonio

Cabo Polonio is a remote and rugged coastal village located within a protected nature reserve on Uruguay's eastern coast. Accessible only by 4x4 vehicles or on foot, Cabo Polonio is renowned for its wild beauty, pristine beaches, and towering sand dunes. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views from the historic lighthouse, observe sea lions basking on the rocks, and stargaze under the clear night sky.

Valizas

Valizas is a charming coastal village located near Cabo Polonio, known for its stunning sand dunes, tranquil beaches, and bohemian atmosphere. Visitors can hike through the shifting sand dunes of the Valizas Natural Reserve, take a boat ride along the nearby Valizas River, or simply relax on the pristine shores of Playa de la Viuda.

Quebrada de los Cuervos

Quebrada de los Cuervos is a breathtaking natural reserve located in the heart of Uruguay's interior, near the town of Treinta y Tres. This stunning canyon landscape features rugged cliffs, lush forests, and cascading waterfalls, offering opportunities for hiking, rock climbing, and birdwatching. Visitors can explore the reserve's scenic trails, picnic beside tranquil streams, and immerse themselves in the tranquility of nature.

Salto del Penitente

Salto del Penitente is a picturesque waterfall located in the scenic hills of Lavalleja Department, near the town of Minas. Nestled amidst verdant forests and rolling hills, this tranquil natural attraction is popular for hiking, picnicking, and swimming in the crystal-clear waters of the natural pool below the waterfall. Visitors can also enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes from the nearby viewpoints.

Cerro Pan de Azúcar

Cerro Pan de Azúcar is Uruguay's second-highest peak, located near the coastal town of Piriápolis. This majestic hill offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding coastline, lush forests, and the vast Atlantic Ocean. Visitors can hike to the summit of Cerro Pan de Azúcar, explore the surrounding nature reserve, and spot diverse flora and fauna along the way

Santa Teresa

Santa Teresa National Park is a vast protected area located along Uruguay's northeastern coast, near the town of Rocha. This coastal paradise features pristine beaches, rolling sand dunes, and dense forests teeming with wildlife. Visitors can explore the park's scenic trails, relax on its secluded beaches, and visit historical attractions such as the Fortaleza de Santa Teresa.

Laguna Garzón

Laguna Garzón is a stunning coastal lagoon located near the town of José Ignacio. Surrounded by sandy beaches and lush wetlands, Laguna Garzón is a haven for birdwatching, kayaking, and stand-up paddleboarding. Visitors can admire the lagoon's tranquil waters, spot migratory birds and native wildlife, and enjoy the serene beauty of this natural oasis.
