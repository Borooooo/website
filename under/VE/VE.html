GO NATURAL

A walk in nauture walks the soul back home

"Every country is beautiful in its own way."

Ecotourism team

Every country is adorned with its own unique tapestry of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical marvels waiting to be explored. From the lush rainforests of Costa Rica to the snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, each nation offers a myriad of breathtaking landscapes to enchant and inspire travelers. Ecotourism has emerged as a powerful force in many countries, encouraging visitors to appreciate and preserve the delicate balance of nature. Whether it's the pristine beaches of the Seychelles or the majestic fjords of Norway, ecotourism destinations provide an opportunity for travelers to connect with the environment in a sustainable and responsible manner. Cultural diversity adds another layer of allure to countries worldwide. From the ancient temples of Angkor Wat in Cambodia to the vibrant markets of Marrakech in Morocco, every corner of the globe is infused with its own distinct heritage and traditions, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the richness of different cultures.

In addition to natural wonders and cultural treasures, the warmth and hospitality of local communities further enhance the beauty of countries around the world. Whether it's savoring homemade cuisine with a local family in Italy or participating in a traditional dance ceremony in Bali, the kindness and generosity of people worldwide leave an indelible mark on travelers' hearts.

Some beauties of Venezuela

Angel Falls

Angel Falls is the world's highest uninterrupted waterfall, plunging over 3,200 feet in Canaima National Park. Surrounded by lush rainforests and towering cliffs, witnessing the majestic cascade of Angel Falls is an awe-inspiring experience. Visitors can take guided tours to admire the falls from various viewpoints or embark on multi-day excursions to explore the park's diverse flora and fauna.

Los Roques Archipelago

The Los Roques Archipelago is a stunning marine park located off the coast of Venezuela. This pristine archipelago comprises over 300 coral islands, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs teeming with marine life. Visitors can snorkel among colorful fish, relax on pristine white-sand beaches, and explore secluded cays and lagoons.

Canaima National Park

Canaima National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its dramatic landscapes, including tepuis (tabletop mountains), vast savannas, and dense rainforests. Visitors can embark on guided tours to explore the park's natural wonders, including the iconic Angel Falls, the Tepui Plateau, and the mesmerizing Sapo Falls.

Mérida Cable Car

The Mérida Cable Car, or Teleférico de Mérida, is the world's longest and highest cable car, offering breathtaking views of the Andean peaks and valleys. As it ascends to Pico Espejo, passengers are treated to panoramic vistas of the surrounding mountains, cloud forests, and the city of Mérida below.

Morrocoy National Park

Morrocoy National Park is a coastal paradise characterized by its pristine beaches, mangrove forests, and turquoise waters. Visitors can explore the park's numerous cays and islets, snorkel among colorful coral reefs, and relax on secluded beaches fringed by palm trees.

Mount Roraima

Mount Roraima is a towering tepui located in the Gran Sabana region, near the borders of Venezuela, Brazil, and Guyana. This ancient tabletop mountain is shrouded in mist and surrounded by sheer cliffs, creating a surreal and otherworldly landscape. Adventurous travelers can embark on multi-day treks to reach the summit and explore its unique ecosystems.

Orinoco Delta

The Orinoco Delta is one of the largest river deltas in the world, characterized by its labyrinthine waterways, mangrove forests, and diverse wildlife. Visitors can take boat tours through the delta's intricate channels, spot exotic birds and wildlife, and experience the traditional way of life of the Warao indigenous people who inhabit the region.

Henri Pittier National Park

Henri Pittier National Park is Venezuela's oldest national park, encompassing diverse ecosystems ranging from coastal mangroves to cloud forests. Visitors can explore the park's numerous hiking trails, discover hidden waterfalls and cascades, and observe an array of flora and fauna, including howler monkeys, colorful birds, and orchids.
